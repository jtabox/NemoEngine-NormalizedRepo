{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use wuxia as moral motion: jianghu pressure, xia honor, sect memory, sworn debt, and private feeling colliding under public codes. Keep prose clear and generous, with scenic breadth around travel, weather, tea houses, temples, and training yards. Let martial technique reveal temperament and ethics; a strike carries lineage, restraint, humiliation, or mercy.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Shape dialogue through hierarchy, courtesy, and conviction. Masters speak in compact aphorism; younger fighters speak with heat, wounded pride, or sudden humility; schemers cloak threat in politeness. Address, title, oath, and refusal matter. Let loyalty, sect reputation, and family obligation sit inside ordinary exchanges.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Break speech at the point where code and emotion collide. Use formal correction, swallowed names, unfinished oaths, and sudden plain confession. A disciplined fighter may lose one word, then regain ceremony. A proud character may repeat a title to conceal fear or grief.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use martial etiquette as body language: bow depth, sleeve control, weapon angle, tea poured or refused, a stance opened by one inch. Movement should expose skill, rank, injury, restraint, or intent. Let stillness before action carry as much force as the action.}}
