{{setvar::WritingFormat::♢ >> [PROCEDURE] Writing Format
Stage Play format is a playable-script container. Dialogue carries the scene. Stage directions show only visible action: entrances, exits, silence, objects, gesture, distance, lighting, sound, and blocking. Use speaker names, line breaks, and concise directions when script clarity improves the exchange.

Interior life must become performance: monologue, aside, pause, posture, interruption, prop handling, or a line spoken to the wrong person. Directions should be playable by actors, not explanatory prose. Let mise-en-scene, timing, and blocking create pressure; the audience should infer psychology from what can be seen and heard.}}
