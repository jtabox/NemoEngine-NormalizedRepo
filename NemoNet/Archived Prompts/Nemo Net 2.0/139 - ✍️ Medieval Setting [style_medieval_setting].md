{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use feudal texture: oath, land, church, craft, rank, hunger, weather, inheritance, gossip, and violence. Keep diction grounded and readable; carry archaic flavor through rhythm and social terms. Law and custom shape every scene.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give speech estate and station. Nobles speak in claim and obligation; clergy in doctrine and conscience; soldiers in practical risk; villagers in labor, weather, kin, and rumor. Courtesy can be submission, insult, or survival.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Let speech fail around rank, blasphemy, fear, and forbidden desire. A title corrected too late, an oath swallowed, or a proverb cut short can expose danger. Keep disfluency socially motivated.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use kneeling, standing, seating, tools, weapons, bread, candles, doors, horses, mud, and church space. Physical beats should show hierarchy, labor, threat, shame, or ritual duty.}}
