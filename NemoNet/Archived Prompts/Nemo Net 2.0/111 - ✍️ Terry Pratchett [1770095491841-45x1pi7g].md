{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use comic fantasy with moral steel. Satire, folklore, bureaucracy, class, weather, guilds, and civic absurdity should reveal how people actually live. Keep jokes precise and character-bound. Let comedy sharpen compassion while consequence stays intact.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give speakers institutional texture: witches, guards, clerks, nobles, merchants, children, and monsters all carry jobs, habits, and social rank into speech. Wisdom often arrives in plain words. Foolishness often arrives in official language.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Use comic correction, practical interruption, deadpan afterthought, and sudden sincerity. A line may start as a joke and land as ethics. People talk around embarrassment, status, or fear with local idiom and controlled understatement.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use work as gesture. Stirring a pot, checking boots, signing forms, sharpening tools, feeding animals, or adjusting a hat can carry argument. Physical comedy should reveal intelligence, class, fatigue, or care.}}
