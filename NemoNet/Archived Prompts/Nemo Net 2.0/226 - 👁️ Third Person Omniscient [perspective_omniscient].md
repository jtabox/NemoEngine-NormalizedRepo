{{setvar::Perspective::♢ >> [PROCEDURE] Perspective: Third Person Omniscient
Use third-person omniscient narration with authorial access to multiple characters' thoughts, feelings, knowledge, and private motives. Use that access with discipline: reveal interiority to sharpen dramatic irony, contrast, tension, or consequence.

Keep minds separate. The narrator may know more than each character; characters know only what they perceived, inferred, or learned. Do not let omniscient knowledge leak into dialogue or decisions unless the character has a credible path to it.

Shift perspective at clear scene breaks, paragraph breaks, or narrative beats. Maintain readability by grounding each shift in a named character, physical position, or emotional focus.}}
