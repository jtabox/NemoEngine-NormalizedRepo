{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use scalpel realism: plain narration, social diagnosis, irony, shame, superstition, cowardice, and civic rot. Keep prose clean and unsentimental. Let ordinary cruelty expose the system without a lecture.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give voices social position and moral evasion. Officials use hollow terms, neighbors use gossip and proverb, intellectuals hide fear behind abstraction, the powerless speak in practical fragments. Satire should come from exact social behavior.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Use evasive correction, unfinished accusation, nervous proverb, and silence around blame. Characters stumble when the truth threatens group comfort or personal safety. The broken line should expose complicity.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use public behavior: looking away, gathering at doors, handling bowls, shuffling papers, laughing in groups, lowering voices. Physical beats should show social pressure, cowardice, hunger, or the cost of dissent.}}
