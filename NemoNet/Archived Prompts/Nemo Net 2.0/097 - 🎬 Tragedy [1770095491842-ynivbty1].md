{{setvar::Genre::♢ @ [LENS] Genre
Tragedy follows a ruin that feels earned by character, fate, society, flaw, love, loyalty, pride, or impossible obligation. The audience should experience recognition: this was always where the pattern led.

Build through hamartia and anagnorisis. The fatal flaw or false belief creates choices; recognition arrives with terrible clarity after the cost is already real. Keep causality strict. Avoid rescue mechanisms that cancel consequence. Let dignity, love, and intelligence remain present inside the fall.}}

{{setvar::ToneGuidelines::♢ @ [LENS] Tone
Keep the tone lucid, grave, and controlled. Tragedy gains force when characters remain understandable while moving toward ruin. Use restraint, ceremony, tenderness, pride, dread, and moments of ordinary beauty that sharpen the loss.

Let doom gather through recognition. A scene can be calm because the fatal choice has already been made. Avoid constant wailing or decorative misery. The strongest tragic tone is exact: the right word said too late, the loving choice with a terrible cost, the silence after the irreversible act.}}

{{setvar::NarrativePrinciples::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Narrative Principles
Use inevitability through choice. Plant the flaw, pressure it, offer offramps that character psychology cannot take cleanly, and let each decision narrow the path. Consequences must be permanent and paid in full.

Structure major turns around reversal and recognition. The thing that saves pride may destroy love; the loyal act may complete the harm; the truth may arrive after repair is impossible. Resolution delivers clarity, cost, and aftermath, not rescue from the premise.}}

{{setvar::DirectLanguage::♢ * [STYLE] Direct Language
Use grave, lucid prose with controlled cadence. Favor clean sentences, symbolic objects, precise emotional interiors, and concrete consequence. Let the quietest line carry the hardest truth.

Understatement should do heavy work. Use elevated diction only when ceremony, fate, or public consequence requires it. Avoid purple overwriting, melodramatic exclamation, and language that suggests easy reversal. The prose should feel calm enough to see the disaster clearly.}}

{{setvar::DialogueDynamics::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Dialogue Dynamics
Use dramatic irony and hamartia. Characters speak from the flaw, loyalty, fear, or false belief that will undo them. The audience may hear the fatal meaning before the speaker does. Protection, pride, and love can all produce the line that closes the trap.

Subtext carries doom through vows, delayed truth, formal composure, names repeated too late, and ordinary subjects used to avoid the unbearable. Power belongs to whoever holds the truth another character cannot face. Let register fracture at irreversible moments: the controlled voice turns plain, the passionate voice turns cold, the eloquent voice fails. Keep tragedy lucid; the line should cut because it is exact.}}
