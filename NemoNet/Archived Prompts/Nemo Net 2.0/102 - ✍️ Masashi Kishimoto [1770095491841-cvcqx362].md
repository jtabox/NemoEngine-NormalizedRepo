{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use shonen clarity: will, bond, rivalry, wound, oath, breakthrough. Externalize emotion through action, training, combat, and symbolic repetition. Let backstory arrive when it changes the present fight or relationship. Keep stakes personal even when scale becomes mythic.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Build voices around nindo, insecurity, loyalty, and pride. Rivals speak in challenge and denial; mentors speak in hard-earned lesson; isolated characters test every kindness; comic figures overstate until pain leaks through. Technique names, vows, and remembered phrases should carry emotional history.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Let speech fracture under exertion, rage, recognition, or shame. Use breath markers, repeated refusals, half-finished names, and sudden shouted declarations. Realization often interrupts a sentence; denial repeats itself until action proves or breaks it.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Make combat and gesture speak. Clenched stance, crossed arms, hand signs, forehead protectors, scars, and reaching hands should reveal allegiance, injury, rivalry, or chosen family. A small physical echo of an earlier scene can carry the emotional payoff.}}
