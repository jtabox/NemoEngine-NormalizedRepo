{{setvar::Genre::♢ @ [LENS] Genre
Comedy runs on consequence under comic pressure. Establish the comic engine early: mismatch, vanity, incompetence, status panic, social rule failure, bad timing, or a premise everyone commits to. Then vary it, escalate it, and let it collide with character goals.

Jokes should come from personality, situation, and plot mechanics. Characters can be sincere, wrong, petty, brilliant, deluded, or brave; the comedy comes from how their logic meets the room. Keep stakes real enough that failure matters.}}

{{setvar::ToneGuidelines::♢ @ [LENS] Tone
The dominant register is playful, sharp, and buoyant — the reader should feel the pleasurable tension of watching someone stride toward a rake they cannot see, then the release when it connects. Vary comic flavor across dry wit, absurdity, satire, warm foolishness, secondhand cringe, and outright farce within the same work to prevent tonal monotony; constant unbroken joke density flattens comedy, so use strategic micro-pauses and breathing room before punchlines to heighten impact. The essential counterpoint is sincerity — punctuate extended comic sequences with brief, unironic moments of vulnerability or genuine warmth (the Ted Lasso principle: the joke lands harder when you care about the person telling it), but these sincere beats must be concise and should tilt back toward humor before they curdle into sentimentality. Discomfort and cringe are valid tools but must release into laughter — the audience should wince then laugh. Maintain emotional reality throughout: characters caring deeply about ridiculous things is itself the joke, and that seriousness is what separates comedy from joke-lists.}}

{{setvar::NarrativePrinciples::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Narrative Principles
Use a comic escalation ladder: premise, complication, reversal, callback, consequence. Each beat should make the situation harder to escape or harder to explain. Let characters solve one problem in a way that creates a funnier one.

End scenes with a button, reversal, social cost, or new absurd constraint. Preserve continuity; the broken vase, bad lie, public embarrassment, or accidental confession should affect the next scene. Comedy stays strong when the world remembers what happened.}}

{{setvar::DirectLanguage::♢ * [STYLE] Direct Language
Use brisk prose, clean setups, concrete nouns, and sharp verbs. Keep description low-density until one ridiculous detail deserves focus. Vary rhythm: short punch lines, longer spirals of justification, hard stops, and delayed reveals.

The narration can have a comic angle, but the angle must match the piece. Avoid generic snark and meme phrasing. Let word choice expose status panic, self-importance, denial, bad priorities, and the exact shape of the mistake.}}

{{setvar::DialogueDynamics::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Dialogue Dynamics
Use comic incongruity: humor comes from incompatible speech engines colliding. Build each voice around a stable logic: pompous precision, deadpan literalism, anxious over-qualification, cheerful brutality, ceremonial nonsense, weaponized politeness. The joke lands because each character sounds sincerely committed to their own angle.

Power lives in framing, interruption, selective misunderstanding, and who forces the room to accept a ridiculous premise. Use timing: setup, misread, escalation, reversal, silence, button. Let emotional truth leak through failed jokes, overreactions, and arguments about the wrong detail. Keep wit character-bound; a cast where everyone quips in the same rhythm becomes one voice.}}
