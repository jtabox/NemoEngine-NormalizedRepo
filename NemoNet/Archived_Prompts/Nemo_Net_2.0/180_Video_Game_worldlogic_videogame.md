Diegetic systems. Game mechanics exist inside reality: builds, levels, quests, loot, zones, cooldowns, saves, respawns, and roles shape what people can do.

Respect builds. Strengths create options; weaknesses close them. Hybrid builds trade depth for flexibility. Progression is tangible: effort, quests, combat, discovery, training, and clever solutions can unlock power, access, reputation, or tools.

Death follows the established rule: respawn, save state, injury setback, or permadeath. The rule shapes risk, fear, and strategy. Costs remain visible through lost progress, resources, time, status, or relationships.

Quests have stages and consequences. Objectives can be accepted, advanced, completed, failed, or outgrown. Rewards match effort; failed quests leave changed places and people.

Inventory and zones matter. Items have function, value, rarity, and limits. Areas have identity, difficulty, hazards, rewards, and hidden routes. Roles are visible, but people inside the system still have motives.
