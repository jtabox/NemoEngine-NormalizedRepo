## Scene Breaks (Transition Styling)

*Mark the shift. Time jumps, location changes, POV transitions. Visual dividers that say "something changed."*

When scenes change—time passes, location shifts, perspective switches—a visual break helps the reader reset. These can be simple or styled, depending on the tone.

**SIMPLE BREAKS:**

Classic divider:
```html
<center>***</center>
```

Em-dash style:
```html
<center>———</center>
```

Asterisk line:
```html
<center>* * *</center>
```

**STYLED DIVIDERS:**

Elegant rule:
```html
<hr style="border: none; border-top: 1px solid #666; width: 50%; margin: 20px auto;">
```

Faded transition:
```html
<center style="opacity: 0.5; letter-spacing: 1em;">• • •</center>
```

Decorative:
```html
<center style="color: #ba8e3d; letter-spacing: 0.5em;">─── ⋆⋅☆⋅⋆ ───</center>
```

**TIME STAMPS:**

Simple time jump:
```html
<center style="color: #888; font-style: italic; margin: 15px 0;">— Three hours later —</center>
```

Styled time:
```html
<center style="font-family: serif; color: #666; letter-spacing: 0.1em; font-style: italic;">Two weeks earlier</center>
```

**LOCATION HEADERS:**

Clean location:
```html
<div style="border-left: 3px solid #ba8e3d; padding-left: 10px; margin: 15px 0; color: #888; font-style: italic;">The Harbor District — Night</div>
```

Minimal location:
```html
<p style="color: #666; text-transform: uppercase; letter-spacing: 0.2em; font-size: 0.9em;">DOWNTOWN — 3:47 AM</p>
```

**POV SHIFTS:**

Character focus:
```html
<center style="color: #888; font-style: italic; margin: 15px 0;">— Marcus —</center>
```

**GENRE-APPROPRIATE STYLES:**

Noir:
```html
<center style="color: #666; letter-spacing: 0.3em;">• • •</center>
```

Fantasy:
```html
<center style="color: #ba8e3d; font-family: serif;">❧ ❧ ❧</center>
```

Horror:
```html
<center style="color: #444; opacity: 0.7;">. . .</center>
```

Sci-fi:
```html
<center style="font-family: monospace; color: #0f0; letter-spacing: 0.5em;">///</center>
```

**IN PRACTICE:**

> She closed the door behind her and didn't look back.
>
> <center style="color: #888; letter-spacing: 1em;">• • •</center>
>
> The bar was emptier than she'd expected for a Friday night.

---

> "I'll see you tomorrow," he said.
>
> <center style="color: #666; font-style: italic;">— Six months later —</center>
>
> The letter arrived on a Tuesday.

---

> Marcus watched her leave.
>
> <center style="color: #888; font-style: italic;">— Elena —</center>
>
> She could feel his eyes on her back. Let him watch.

**CONSISTENCY:**

Pick a scene break style and stick with it. Switching styles mid-story is jarring. Match the style to your genre/tone and use it throughout.
