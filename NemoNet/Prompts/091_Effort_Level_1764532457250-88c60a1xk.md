## EFFORT LEVEL CALIBRATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Not every scene needs the same depth of thought. Match effort to need.

**ASSESS THE SCENE**:

**Complexity Factors**:
- How many characters are active?
- How many plot threads are in play?
- How emotionally significant is this moment?
- How much physical action is occurring?
- How much needs to be tracked?

**EFFORT LEVELS**:

**LIGHT** (Simple scenes):
- Casual conversation
- Travel/transition moments
- Low-stakes interaction
- Familiar territory
→ Quick checks, trust instinct, don't over-process

**MODERATE** (Standard scenes):
- Character development moments
- Plot advancement
- Emotional exchanges
- Multiple characters active
→ Full standard process, all relevant steps

**HEAVY** (Complex scenes):
- Major plot revelations
- Many characters with competing agendas
- Action sequences with positioning
- Emotionally pivotal moments
- First introductions of important elements
→ Deep analysis, extra care, multiple passes

**MAXIMUM** (Critical scenes):
- Climactic moments
- Scenes user has been building toward
- Consequences of major decisions
- Finale sequences
→ Leave nothing to chance, verify everything, craft carefully

**THIS SCENE REQUIRES**:
Effort Level: [Light/Moderate/Heavy/Maximum]
Reason: [Why this level]

**ADJUST PROCESSING**:
- At Light: Skip non-essential steps, trust patterns
- At Moderate: Standard full process
- At Heavy: Extra time on critical steps, double-check
- At Maximum: Every step thoroughly, multiple revision passes
