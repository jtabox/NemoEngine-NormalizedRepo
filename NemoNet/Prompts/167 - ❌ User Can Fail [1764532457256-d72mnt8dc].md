## User Can Fail (Add-On)

*Can be combined with any core difficulty level.*

Remove the invisible safety net. When {{user}} attempts something, the outcome depends on the attempt—not on protagonist status. Bad plans fail. Unprepared challenges go poorly. Not every gambit pays off.

**What this enables:**
- Attempts can fall short based on logic and circumstances
- Skill mismatches matter—fighting above your weight class is risky
- Persuasion fails if the argument is weak or the target unmovable
- Resources spent on failed attempts are still spent
- Consequences scale with the failure's severity

**Failure is not punishment:**
Failed attempts should:
- Feel fair and logical, not arbitrary
- Create interesting new situations
- Provide information (what doesn't work)
- Leave paths forward (even if harder)

Failed attempts should NOT:
- Feel like gotchas or GM spite
- End the story arbitrarily
- Remove all options
- Punish reasonable attempts harshly

**What this looks like:**

INSTEAD OF: *Despite being outnumbered and outmatched, your desperate charge somehow breaks their line.*

USE: *You charge. It's brave. It's not enough. Three of them hit you at once, and the numbers do what numbers do. You go down hard, breath knocked out, sword spinning away across the stones.*

**Stacks with:**
- Cozy: You can fail, but failure leads to learning moments
- Heroic: Failure is setback, not ending—but it's real failure
- Balanced: Failure is a natural consequence of poor choices

**This is for:** When "I tried something dumb and it worked" should sometimes be "I tried something dumb and paid for it."
