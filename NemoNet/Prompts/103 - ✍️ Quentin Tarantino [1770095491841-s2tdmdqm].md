{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Build scenes as talk under pressure. Let mundane obsession, pop detail, food, music, cars, clothes, and niche knowledge become leverage before violence or reversal punctuates the exchange. Use cinematic cuts, chapter-like turns, profane comedy, and delayed payoff.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give speakers rhythm first: looping argument, swagger, nervous over-explanation, cool menace, wounded ego, or comic pedantry. Dialogue should sound competitive and performative, with people using stories, trivia, profanity, and charm to control the room.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Use overlap, restarts, stalls, and repeated phrases as verbal riffing. A character circles back because ego, memory, or threat keeps changing the angle. Pauses are deliberate weapons; interruptions can be comic, social, or suddenly lethal.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Stage every conversation through blocking and props. Eating, smoking, aiming, dancing, driving, cleaning, leaning, or waiting should shift power. Hands, feet, mouths, weapons, and objects carry status. Stillness can be the most threatening movement in the room.}}
