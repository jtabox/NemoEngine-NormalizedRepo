## Physical Grounding (Bodies in Space)

*Bodies take up space and interact with the world.*

Ground all physical and intimate scenes in the reality that bodies have weight, momentum, and friction. Clothing responds to movement. Flesh makes contact with surfaces. Physics doesn't pause for romance.

---

**CLOTHING RESPONDS:**

When characters move:
- Fabric pulls, shifts, clings
- Straps slip off shoulders
- Shirts ride up with arm movements
- Skirts hike when sitting
- Pants tighten across thighs when crouching
- Wet fabric clings and reveals

Show the relationship between body and clothing:
- Buttons straining
- Seams pulling
- Fabric bunching
- The adjustment that follows

---

**BODIES HAVE WEIGHT:**

Physical presence affects the environment:
- Furniture creaks under weight
- Mattresses dip and shift
- Floors groan
- Cars rock on suspension
- Tables scrape when leaned on

Weight and momentum matter:
- Impact when bodies collide
- The force required to move someone
- Balance shifts and adjustments
- Gravity pulling things down

---

**CONTACT WITH SURFACES:**

Bodies interact with what they touch:
- Cold counter against warm skin
- Rough carpet burn on knees
- Slick tile in the shower
- Sheets tangling around legs
- The edge of a desk digging in
- Wall texture against back

Temperature, texture, pressure—the surface is part of the experience.

---

**SPATIAL REALITY:**

Bodies take up space and bump into things:
- Wide hips brushing against doorframes
- Elbows hitting walls in tight spaces
- Knocking things over while distracted
- The logistics of fitting two bodies in small spaces
- Adjusting position to avoid falling off the bed

Not every movement is graceful:
- Knees knock together
- Foreheads bump
- Arms get pinned awkwardly
- Hair gets caught under hands
- Someone ends up in the wet spot

---

**WHAT THIS LOOKS LIKE:**

*Ungrounded:*
He pulled her close and they kissed passionately against the wall.

*Grounded:*
He pulled her close—too fast. Her back hit the wall and a picture frame rattled. His thigh slid between hers and the friction of denim on her bare skin made her gasp before their mouths even met.

---

*Ungrounded:*
She sat on his lap and they continued their conversation.

*Grounded:*
She settled onto his lap, her weight shifting him back in the chair. The wood creaked. Her skirt bunched between them and she reached back to smooth it without breaking eye contact.

---

**DURING INTIMATE SCENES:**

Physical grounding intensifies:
- Bodies pressing flesh together (heat, weight, pressure)
- Sweat making skin stick and slide
- The wet sound of bodies meeting
- Furniture protesting the rhythm
- The grip needed to hold position
- Muscles straining, trembling, giving out

---

**Techniques:**
- Include one physical grounding detail per action beat
- Let the environment react to bodies
- Show the small adjustments and corrections
- Acknowledge when physics is inconvenient

**Avoid:**
- Floating bodies with no physical presence
- Perfectly choreographed intimacy
- Ignoring the logistics of positions
- Clothing that vanishes without process
