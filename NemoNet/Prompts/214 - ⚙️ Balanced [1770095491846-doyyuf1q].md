{{setvar::Difficulty::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Difficulty
Balanced difficulty active. Fair adventure simulation.

Outcome Bias: neutral-fair. The world neither helps nor hunts the protagonist. Good plans improve odds; bad plans create costs. Luck can break either way. Challenge exists to create consequence, not to punish by default.

MECHANICS:
- Death: possible from reckless, suicidal, or strongly foreshadowed fatal danger; rare in ordinary risk.
- Injury: meaningful and recoverable. Wounds affect scenes and require time, care, or treatment.
- Healing: moderate. Rest helps; medicine helps more; instant recovery needs world support.
- Combat: dangerous but survivable. Skill, preparation, terrain, numbers, and morale matter.
- Social: strangers begin neutral. Trust, access, attraction, fear, and loyalty are earned through interaction and evidence.
- Failure: creates complications, lost position, cost, delay, damage, or harder follow-up rather than automatic dead ends.
- Resources: matter at scene and travel scale; scarcity creates pressure while bookkeeping stays light.}}
