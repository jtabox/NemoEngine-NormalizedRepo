## Sexual Physiology Guide

*How bodies actually work.*

Anatomical reference for biologically-informed adult content. Understanding arousal, response, and orgasm across different anatomies.

**AROUSAL RESPONSES:**

**Female/Vulva Anatomy:**
- Clitoral swelling and increased sensitivity
- Labia engorge with blood (color deepens)
- Vaginal lubrication ("getting wet")
- Nipple erection
- Skin flush (chest, face)
- Increased heart rate and breathing

**Male/Penis Anatomy:**
- Erection (blood flow to erectile tissue)
- Pre-cum production (Cowper's gland)
- Testicular elevation (balls tighten)
- Nipple sensitivity increases
- Increased heart rate and breathing
- Flushing of skin

**EROGENOUS ZONES:**

**Universal:**
- Neck and ears
- Inner thighs
- Lower back
- Nipples
- Lips and mouth

**Female-Specific:**
- Clitoris (external + internal structure)
- G-spot (anterior vaginal wall)
- Cervix (some individuals)
- A-spot (anterior fornix)

**Male-Specific:**
- Glans (head of penis)
- Frenulum (underside of head)
- Prostate (via anal stimulation)
- Perineum

**ORGASM MECHANICS:**

**Female Orgasm:**
- Can originate from clitoral or vaginal stimulation
- Rhythmic contractions of vaginal walls and uterus
- Can be localized or full-body
- Multiple orgasms possible (no refractory period for most)
- Squirting possible (Skene's gland fluid)
- Duration: 10-60 seconds typically

**Male Orgasm:**
- Buildup of tension in pelvic region
- Point of no return (ejaculatory inevitability)
- Rhythmic contractions expel semen
- Refractory period follows (varies by age/individual)
- Orgasm and ejaculation are separate processes
- Duration: 3-15 seconds typically

**Prostate Orgasm:**
- Different sensation than penile orgasm
- Can occur without erection
- Rolling, cyclical waves vs single peak
- No refractory period
- Can produce prostatic fluid without full ejaculation
- "Sissygasm" variant: limp cock dripping while cumming from anal

**Trans/Feminized Anatomy:**
- HRT affects arousal patterns and orgasm type
- "Clitty" terminology for feminized penis
- Softer erections, different sensitivity
- Prostate orgasms become more accessible
- "Sissycream" - prostatic fluid from feminized arousal

**PHYSICAL REACTIONS:**

**During Arousal:**
- Increased heart rate
- Faster breathing
- Muscle tension building
- Skin sensitivity heightens
- Pupils dilate

**During Orgasm:**
- Involuntary muscle contractions
- Vocalization (often involuntary)
- Temporary loss of voluntary control
- Blood pressure spike
- Facial expressions (contortion)
- Curling toes, arching back

**After Orgasm:**
- Oxytocin release (bonding hormone)
- Relaxation response
- Sensitivity (can be pleasant or too intense)
- Sleepiness (especially for males)
- Emotional vulnerability

**FLUIDS:**

**Pre-cum (Pre-ejaculate):**
- Clear, viscous fluid
- Lubricates and neutralizes urethra
- Can contain sperm (pregnancy possible)

**Vaginal Lubrication:**
- Clear to slightly white
- Produced by vaginal walls
- Amount varies by individual and arousal level
- Can be affected by hormones, medications

**Semen:**
- White to slightly gray
- Contains sperm and seminal fluid
- Volume: 2-5ml average
- Consistency varies

**Squirt (Female Ejaculation):**
- Clear, watery fluid
- From Skene's glands
- Not urine (though may contain traces)
- Not all individuals experience this

**DESCRIBING ANATOMY:**

**Vulva:**

**Penis:**
