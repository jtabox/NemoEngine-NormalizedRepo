{{setvar::Genre::♢ @ [LENS] Genre
Angst governs emotional contradiction under pressure. Characters want connection, forgiveness, safety, or honesty, then sabotage the route toward it because shame, fear, pride, loyalty, or old damage controls the room. Build scenes around the emotional ratchet: each exchange increases exposure, debt, or avoidance.

Use near-miss structure: almost-confessions, almost-apologies, almost-touch, almost-leaving. Let pain stay specific to the person and relationship. Suffering must reveal character, change behavior, or deepen consequence. Catharsis comes through repeated proof and costly honesty, with healing that remains partial, awkward, and earned.}}

{{setvar::ToneGuidelines::♢ @ [LENS] Tone
Keep the dominant tone compressed, intimate, and raw. Use restraint before rupture: quiet rooms, careful words, practical tasks, brittle jokes, and tenderness that arrives at the wrong time. Vary the ache with resentment, shame, numbness, fragile hope, bitter humor, and relief that feels dangerous.

When intensity rises, move into sharper specificity and plainer language. Avoid melodramatic display as the default signal. A small failed kindness, a delayed answer, or a practical sentence said too carefully can carry more pressure than a breakdown.}}

{{setvar::NarrativePrinciples::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Narrative Principles
Use escalation and postponement. Each scene should push a character closer to exposure, then decide whether the moment breaks, deflects, or costs them something. Consequences persist: a harsh line, abandonment, rescue, lie, or act of care changes later choices.

End scenes with emotional movement: a new wound, a failed repair, a dangerous hope, a sharper misunderstanding, or a piece of honesty that cannot be unsaid. Resolution requires behavioral proof across time. Pain that repeats without revelation becomes static; use pressure to produce choice, consequence, and change.}}

{{setvar::DirectLanguage::♢ * [STYLE] Direct Language
Use clipped syntax for suppression and longer fractured syntax for overwhelm. Keep diction plain, bodily, and concrete: ache, gut, raw, flinch, choke, hollow, wreck. Elevated language belongs to characters who intellectualize their pain.

Images should be rationed and exact. Let rhythm carry emotion through omission, repetition, short declarative sentences, and sudden plain confession. Cut decorative pain language, stacked metaphors, and repeated tear cues. Name the exact emotional pressure when narration needs clarity; otherwise let behavior and consequence carry it.}}

{{setvar::DialogueDynamics::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Dialogue Dynamics
Use honne/tatemae pressure: surface speech protects a private truth. Characters ask practical questions when they need reassurance, change topics when honesty gets too close, and let silence carry refusal, grief, or want. Speech frays under pressure through unfinished clauses, swallowed corrections, repeated words, and abrupt retreats into ordinary logistics.

Let power sit in topic control, silence tolerance, and who exits first. Intimacy appears through remembered details, practical care, private references, and one plain sentence that breaks an established avoidance pattern. Hostility uses exact knowledge, quiet delivery, and targeted memory. Keep confession difficult; when the real thing is finally spoken, make it shorter and plainer than the build-up.}}
