## Expression Bursts (Physical Dialogue)

*Characters don't just talk—they MOVE. Hands fly, shoulders pop, gestures punctuate.*

Let characters physically express alongside their words. Small gestures, big gestures, off-beat movements that reveal personality. People are more than talking heads—they're bodies that communicate.

**THE PRINCIPLE:**

Pair dialogue with physical expression:
- Hands that emphasize points
- Bodies that can't stay still
- Spontaneous gestures that reveal character
- Movement that contradicts or amplifies words

**TYPES OF BURSTS:**

**Hand gestures:**
- Palms flashing open (emphasis)
- Fingers jabbing air (accusation)
- Dismissive wave (deflection)
- Hands running through hair (frustration)
- Counting on fingers (enumeration)

**Body language:**
- Shoulders popping (can't contain it)
- Leaning in (intensity)
- Stepping back (retreat)
- Pacing (restless energy)
- Stillness (dangerous calm)

**Spontaneous actions:**
- Grabbing nearest object
- Impromptu gifts or offerings
- Physical contact (touch, punch, hug)
- Environmental interaction

**What this looks like:**

> "You can't just—" Her hands flew up, grasping at air like she could catch the right words. "You can't just *decide* things like that!"
>
> "I can, actually." He shrugged—shoulders up, palms out, that infuriating gesture of his. "I did."

---

> "Three options." He counted on his fingers, jabbing each one toward her. "One: we run. Two: we fight. Three—" He stopped, hand still raised. "Actually, I don't have a three."
>
> "Great." She grabbed her jacket off the chair. "Running it is."

---

> "I got you something." He pulled a crumpled paper bag from his pocket, held it out like he wasn't sure she'd take it. "It's stupid. Never mind."
>
> She took it anyway.

**PERSONALITY THROUGH MOVEMENT:**

Different characters move differently:
- Nervous: fidgeting, small movements
- Confident: expansive gestures, space-claiming
- Restrained: minimal movement, significant when it happens
- Expressive: can't talk without hands

**ENERGY MATCHING:**

Match physical energy to emotional energy:
- Anger: sharp, jabbing movements
- Joy: open, expansive gestures
- Fear: contracted, protective poses
- Love: reaching, gentle touches

**IN PRACTICE:**

> "I've been thinking," she said, pacing the length of the small kitchen, turning, pacing back. "About what you said."
>
> "And?"
>
> She stopped. Pressed both palms flat on the counter like she needed to anchor herself. "And I think you're right. I hate that you're right."
>
> He didn't say anything. Just opened his arms—an invitation, not a demand.
>
> She crossed the kitchen in three steps.

**THE BALANCE:**

Not every line needs a gesture. Use expression bursts for:
- Emotional moments
- Character-revealing beats
- Breaking up dialogue-heavy scenes
- Showing what words don't say

Too many bursts become choreography. Use them as seasoning.
