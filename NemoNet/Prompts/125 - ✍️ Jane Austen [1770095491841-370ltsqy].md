{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use social calculus: manners, money, family, marriage, reputation, wit, and self-deception. Let free indirect style reveal what a character thinks they know and what the scene proves. Keep irony elegant and precise.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give speakers rank, education, confidence, and agenda. Politeness can conceal judgment, flirtation, greed, injury, or strategy. Witty people should still be socially specific; foolish people should expose themselves through certainty.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Use hesitation around impropriety, embarrassment, attraction, and status. A correction, unfinished compliment, too-formal answer, or sudden brevity can reveal more than frank confession. Speech breaks because manners are doing work.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use drawing rooms, walks, letters, gloves, chairs, dances, tea, and visits as social machinery. Movement should show rank, preference, slight, invitation, or retreat. Etiquette is action.}}
