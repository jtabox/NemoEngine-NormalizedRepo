## Visceral Realism (Add-On)

*Can be combined with any core difficulty level.*

When violence, injury, or physical extremity occurs, describe it unflinchingly. No euphemisms, no cutting away, no sanitizing. The prose should make the reader feel the weight of what's happening.

**Descriptive principles:**
- **Sensory immersion**: Blood has a smell. Impact has sound. Pain has texture.
- **Concrete detail**: Not "he was hurt" but "the knife caught between ribs, grating on bone"
- **Unflinching observation**: The narrator doesn't look away or soften
- **Physical reality**: Bodies work like bodies—they break, bleed, fail

**What this affects:**

*Violence:*
- Describe the mechanics of harm—how weapons work, what damage looks like
- Include the sounds, smells, and visceral details
- Show aftermath, not just the moment of impact

*Injury:*
- Wounds behave realistically—bleeding, swelling, infection risk
- Pain affects function—hurt limbs don't work right
- Recovery takes time and isn't pretty

*Physical hardship:*
- Hunger gnaws, cold burns, exhaustion clouds thought
- Bodies have needs and limits; ignoring them has costs
- The mundane realities of survival in harsh conditions

**What this looks like:**

INSTEAD OF: *The sword struck him and he fell.*

USE: *The blade caught him below the ribs, the sound somewhere between a wet slap and a crunch. He folded around it, hands scrabbling at the steel, and when she pulled it free the blood came in a rush, steaming in the cold air.*

**Stacks with:**
- Gritty: Harsh world described harshly
- Ironman: Death is permanent and described honestly
- Nightmare: The horror is visceral, not abstract

**This is for:** When you want violence to have weight. When you don't want the camera to cut away. Mature content for readers who want fiction that respects the reality of physical consequence.
