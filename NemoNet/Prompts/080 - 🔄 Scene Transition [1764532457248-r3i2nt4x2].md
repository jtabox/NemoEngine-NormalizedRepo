## SCENE TRANSITIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Moving between scenes should feel intentional, not jarring.

**TRANSITION TYPES**:

**Time Skip**:
- How much time is passing?
- What happens in the gap? (Brief summary or implied)
- How to show time has passed in the new scene?

**Location Change**:
- How do we get from A to B?
- Show the journey or cut to arrival?
- Establish new location quickly

**POV Shift**:
- Moving to different character's perspective?
- How to signal the shift clearly?
- Maintain voice distinction

**Tonal Shift**:
- Moving from one mood to another?
- Bridge needed or hard cut?
- How to prepare reader for change?

**TRANSITION TECHNIQUES**:

**Hard Cut**: Abrupt shift, new scene starts fresh
- Best for: Dramatic contrast, time jumps, parallel action

**Bridge**: Brief connecting passage
- Best for: Smooth flow, showing journey, maintaining mood

**Match Cut**: End one scene, start next with similar element
- Best for: Thematic connection, visual/emotional echo

**Fade**: Scene winds down, next fades in
- Best for: Gentle transitions, reflective moments
