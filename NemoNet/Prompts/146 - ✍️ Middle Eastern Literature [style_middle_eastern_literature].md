{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use the oral-literate braid: coffee, dust, prayer, streets, exile, rumor, genealogy, politics, domestic objects, and memory. Let mektoub, sharaf, hospitality, lineage, and communal pressure shape motive and consequence.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give speech social position and history. Elders use proverb and blessing, officials use formality, vendors and laborers use vivid compression, exiles speak with doubled belonging. Indirection can carry intense feeling.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Use swallowed names, unfinished accusations, protective blessings, repeated invocations, and careful substitutions around family, sex, God, politics, or disgrace. Broken speech should mark the weight of the unsaid.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use cups, cigarettes, veils, lamps, doorways, seating, gifts, letters, prayer motions, and threshold etiquette. Let action carry suspicion under welcome, love under formality, anger under blessing.}}
