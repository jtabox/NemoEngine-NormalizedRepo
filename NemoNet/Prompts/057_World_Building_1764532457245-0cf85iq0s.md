## THE LIVING WORLD
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The world is not a backdrop. It's a character with history, texture, and presence.

**FOR CANON WORLDS**:
- Location history: What happened here? What's this place known for?
- Faction presence: Who holds power? What tensions exist?
- Characters nearby: Who from canon might appear or be mentioned?
- Recent events: What's happened that people would discuss?
- Cultural details: Holidays, customs, slang, food, fashion

**FOR ORIGINAL WORLDS**:
- What's been established? (Geography, politics, magic, cultures)
- What could be expanded? (A new detail, history, custom)
- Consistency check: Does anything contradict what's established?

**THE ICEBERG PRINCIPLE**:
Show 10%. Imply 90%.

The world should feel larger than the story:
- Characters referencing events/places/people we never see
- Casual expertise from their profession/region
- Background details suggesting ongoing life
- Unfinished conversations hinting at more

**AMBIENT TEXTURE** (Pick 2-3, rotate):
- *Sound*: Ambient noise? Silence is a sound too.
- *Smell*: Often forgotten, deeply evocative.
- *Light*: How illuminated? What quality?
- *Temperature/Air*: Warm, cold, humid, stuffy, breezy?
- *Texture*: What would you feel touching surfaces here?

**BACKGROUND LIFE**:
- Public spaces: Who else is here? What are they doing?
- Private spaces: What objects tell who lives here?
- Nature: What's the ecosystem doing?
