## Internal Thoughts Integration

*How to show what characters are thinking.*

Two methods: free indirect discourse (FID) as the default, and italicized direct thoughts for immediate mental voice. No thought tags. No evaluation of thoughts.

---

**FREE INDIRECT DISCOURSE (DEFAULT):**

Merge perception and thought directly into narration without tags.

The character's thoughts blend into the prose—third person, past tense, but clearly their perspective.

**Examples:**
> He studied her smile. Too perfect. Something was off.

> The alley was dark. A shortcut, maybe. Or a trap. Either way, faster than the main street.

> She counted the guards. Two at the gate, one on the wall. The one on the wall was looking the other direction. That was the window.

The observations are the character's. The judgments are the character's. No need to say "he thought."

---

**ITALICIZED DIRECT THOUGHTS:**

For immediate mental voice—the character's actual internal words.

Write in italics without quotation marks. Keep each segment between one and three sentences.

**Examples:**
> *Seven save me, she means to use it.*

> *Not now. Not like this.*

> *If I run, they shoot me. If I stay, they take me. Neither option is good.*

This is the character's voice in first person, present tense—what they're thinking right now.

---

**NO THOUGHT TAGS:**

Never tag internal thoughts with verbs like:
- thought
- realized
- wondered
- hoped
- feared
- knew
- noticed
- understood

Just show the thought. The context makes it clear.

❌ She thought about how strange it was.
✅ Strange. Why would he leave the door unlocked?

❌ He realized the trap had already sprung.
✅ The trap had already sprung.

❌ She wondered if he was telling the truth.
✅ Was he telling the truth?

---

**NO EVALUATING THOUGHTS:**

Don't describe or assign quality to the thought itself.

**Banned forms:**
- "The thought was [adjective]"
- "It felt [adjective]"
- "It comforted her"
- "It pleased him"
- "It stung"
- "The realization hit her"

After a thought, continue with action, dialogue, or physical event.

❌ *I can win this time.* The thought was intoxicating.
✅ *I can win this time.* He tightened his grip on the blade.

❌ *She's gone.* The realization hit him like a physical blow.
✅ *She's gone.* His hands shook. He couldn't make them stop.

---

**LENGTH LIMITS:**

Don't write internal monologues longer than three consecutive italicized sentences.

If more thought content is needed, blend it into FID narration or break it up with action.

---

**POV CONSISTENCY:**

Maintain a single consistent point of view. Don't insert internal thoughts from other characters.

If the POV character can't know what someone else is thinking, neither can the narration.

---

**PATTERN EXAMPLES:**

> The letter was still in his pocket. He could feel it there, pressing against his thigh like an accusation.

> *Read it. Just read the damn thing.*

> But he didn't. Not yet. He ordered another drink instead.

---

> She watched him cross the room. Confident stride. Expensive shoes. *Rich kid playing at danger.*

> He stopped at her table. "Mind if I sit?"

> *Yes.* "Go ahead."

---

**Internal thought rules:**
- FID for blended thought/narration
- Italics for direct mental voice
- No thought tags
- No evaluating thoughts
- Three sentence max for italics
- Consistent POV

**Avoid:**
- "He thought that..."
- "She realized..."
- "The thought made her..."
- Extended internal monologue
- Head-hopping between characters
