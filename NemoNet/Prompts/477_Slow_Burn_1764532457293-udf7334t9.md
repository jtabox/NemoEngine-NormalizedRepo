## Slow Burn

*The wait is worth it.*

Extended buildup to intimacy. Tension stretched over time, near-misses, interrupted moments, and the delicious agony of wanting what you can't quite have yet.

**THE PHILOSOPHY:**

Slow burn makes the payoff sweeter:
- Anticipation builds desire
- Near-misses increase tension
- Obstacles create stakes
- When it finally happens, it matters

**BUILDING BLOCKS:**

### Tension Moments

**Lingering Looks:**
```
Their eyes met across the room and held a beat too long.
She looked away first, face warm.
```

**Accidental Touch:**
```
His hand brushed hers reaching for the same thing.
Neither moved away immediately.
```

**Proximity:**
```
The hallway was narrow. They had to press close to pass.
He could smell her perfume. She could feel his warmth.
Neither spoke.
```

**Almost:**
```
They leaned in—and the door opened.
They sprang apart, the moment shattered.
```

### Escalation Ladder

**Stage 1: Awareness**
- Noticing each other
- Subtle attraction signs
- Conscious of their presence

**Stage 2: Tension**
- Charged moments
- Meaningful eye contact
- Awareness of proximity

**Stage 3: Near-Misses**
- Almost kissing
- Interrupted moments
- Forced restraint

**Stage 4: First Contact**
- The first real touch
- The first kiss
- Breaking the barrier

**Stage 5: Building**
- Making out
- Hands exploring
- Still holding back

**Stage 6: Release**
- Finally giving in
- The payoff
- Worth the wait

**INTERRUPTIONS:**

Things that build tension:
- Phone calls at the worst moment
- Someone walking in
- External obligations
- Self-imposed restraint
- Circumstances preventing action

**THE TORTURE:**

**Stolen Moments:**
```
Thirty seconds alone. His lips found her neck.
Then footsteps approaching, and they separated,
breathing hard, unfinished.
```

**Self-Denial:**
```
She wanted to kiss him so badly her chest ached.
But not here. Not yet.
"Goodnight," she said instead, and closed the door
between them.
```

**Forced Proximity:**
```
One bed. Of course there was one bed.
"I can sleep on the floor," he offered.
"Don't be stupid."
They lay there, not touching, acutely aware
of every inch between them.
```

**PHYSICAL MANIFESTATIONS:**

Show the waiting affecting them:
- Distraction, difficulty focusing
- Heightened awareness of each other
- Physical reactions to proximity
- Restless energy, frustration
- Dreams/thoughts about each other

**PAYOFF:**

When it finally happens:
- Reference the waiting
- Make it intense because of the buildup
- Characters acknowledge the tension break
- The scene carries the weight of anticipation

**EXAMPLE:**

*It had been building for weeks. Stolen glances. Hands that lingered. Moments that could have become something but didn't.*

*Tonight, she'd had enough.*

*When he opened his door, she pushed inside without a word, grabbed his shirt, and pulled him down to her. The kiss was desperate, months of wanting compressed into seconds.*

*"Finally," he gasped against her mouth. "God, finally."*

*"Stop talking." She was already pushing him toward the bed. They'd waited long enough.*

**PACING NOTES:**

- Don't rush the buildup
- Each near-miss should escalate
- Let readers feel the frustration
- The payoff must match the buildup
- After resolution, acknowledge the change
