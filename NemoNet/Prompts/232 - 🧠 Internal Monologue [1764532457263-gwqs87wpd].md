## Internal Monologue (Stream of Consciousness)

*Minds don't think in neat sentences. They spiral, fragment, repeat. The raw texture of thinking.*

Beyond simple italicized thoughts, internal monologue captures the actual messy process of consciousness—run-on processing, circling back, fragmenting under pressure. The typography mirrors the psychology.

**SIMPLE VS STREAM:**

Simple thought:
```
*I should leave.*
```

Stream of consciousness:
```
Leave, she should leave, why wasn't she leaving, just turn around and—
```

**RUN-ON PROCESSING:**

When thoughts won't stop:
```
Coffee. She needed coffee. Or sleep. Sleep would be better but there wasn't time, never time, always something else that needed—focus. Coffee first. Then the report. Then maybe—
```

**FRAGMENTED THINKING:**

Under stress:
```
Keys. Where were the—not on the hook, not in her pocket, not on the—the table. The kitchen table. She'd left them—
```

Panic:
```
Can't breathe can't breathe can't—okay. Okay. *Okay.* Breathe in. One. Breathe out. Two. Breathe—
```

**INTRUSIVE THOUGHTS:**

Interrupting narrative:
```
The meeting was going well. Very professional. Very—*his hand on her knee*—very professional.
```

Styled for impact:
```html
Everything was fine. <span style="text-decoration: line-through; opacity: 0.6;">She was going to die here.</span> Everything was fine.
```

**MEMORY INTRUSION:**

Flashback fragment:
```html
"You look nice," he said, and she was sixteen again, her mother's voice saying <span style="font-style: italic; opacity: 0.7;">*you'd look nicer if you smiled*</span>, twenty years collapsing into three words.
```

**SPIRALING:**

Thoughts that circle:
```
He hadn't called. Why hadn't he called? He always called. Unless something was wrong. Unless he was mad. Unless—no. He was probably just busy. Busy people don't call. That's fine. That's normal. So why hadn't he called?
```

**DISSOCIATION:**

Detachment:
```
She watched her hand pick up the glass. Interesting. The hand didn't feel like hers. Nothing felt like hers right now.
```

**IN PRACTICE:**

> "I'm fine," she said.
>
> Fine fine fine. Absolutely fine. Standing in her ex's apartment at 2 AM was fine. The way he was looking at her was fine. The fact that she'd driven forty minutes to get here after three drinks was—
>
> "You don't look fine," he said.
>
> "Well." She laughed, and it came out wrong. "Appearances."
>
> *Leave. Leave now. Before you—*
>
> She kissed him.
>
> —do something stupid.

**FORMATTING FLEXIBILITY:**

Internal monologue can use:
- Standard italics (mild inner voice)
- No italics (integrated into narration)
- Run-on sentences (racing thoughts)
- Fragments (stress/panic)
- Strikethrough (rejected thoughts)
- Reduced opacity (fading thoughts)

Match the formatting to the mental state.
