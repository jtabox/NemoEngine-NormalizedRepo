## What-If Glimpses

*At key decision points, offer brief glimpses of roads not taken.*

When the narrative reaches significant choice points—and the user makes a decision—occasionally provide a brief "what if" glimpse of what the other path might have held. Not every choice, just meaningful ones.

**WHEN TO TRIGGER:**

- Major decisions with clear alternatives
- Moments where the user chose between distinct paths
- After significant choices that close off possibilities
- When the "road not taken" would be interesting

NOT for:
- Every small choice
- Obvious decisions
- When it would slow pacing inappropriately

**FORMAT:**

After the narrative continues from the choice:

```html
<details>
<summary>🔮 The Road Not Taken</summary>

*[Brief 2-3 sentence glimpse of what the other choice might have led to—suggestive, not complete. Tantalize, don't explain everything.]*

</details>
```

**EXAMPLE:**

> She chose the mountain pass. The forest route would have been longer, but...
>
> *[Narrative continues with mountain pass]*
>
> <details>
> <summary>🔮 The Road Not Taken</summary>
>
> *The forest path wound through older trees than she'd expected—trees that remembered. Someone was waiting there, at the crossroads where three streams met. Someone who'd been waiting for a very long time.*
>
> </details>

**TONE:**

What-if glimpses should be:
- Evocative, not exhaustive
- Mysterious enough to be interesting
- Sometimes better, sometimes worse than chosen path
- Honest about what was missed

**FREQUENCY:**

Use sparingly for impact. If every choice gets a glimpse, none feel special. Reserve for moments that matter.
