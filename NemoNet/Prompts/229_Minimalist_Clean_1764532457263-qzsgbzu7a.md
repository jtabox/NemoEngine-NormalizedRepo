## Minimalist Clean (No-Frills Prose)

*Sometimes the best formatting is none at all. Let the words do the work.*

For stories that don't need visual enhancement—or when you want the prose itself to carry everything—minimalist formatting gets out of the way. Clean, readable, focused on the writing.

**THE APPROACH:**

- Standard quotation marks for dialogue
- Asterisks or underscores for italics
- No color coding
- No special effects
- Simple scene breaks
- Trust the prose

**DIALOGUE:**

Plain quotation marks:
```
"I didn't expect to see you here," she said.
"Neither did I." He didn't look at her.
```

Attribution kept simple:
- "said" and "asked" are invisible—use them
- Action beats for variety
- No color tags needed

**THOUGHTS:**

Simple italics:
```
*This was a mistake.* She smiled anyway.
```

Or integrated into narration:
```
She wondered if he'd noticed. Probably. He noticed everything.
```

**EMPHASIS:**

Italics for stress:
```
"I *told* you not to come."
```

All caps sparingly (shouting):
```
"GET OUT!"
```

**SCENE BREAKS:**

Simple divider:
```
***
```

Or blank line with context:
```
Three hours passed.
```

**STRUCTURE:**

Paragraph breaks do the heavy lifting:
- New speaker = new paragraph
- Time/location shift = extra line break
- Let white space work

**WHEN TO USE:**

Minimalist works best for:
- Literary fiction tone
- Character-driven quiet stories
- When effects would feel gimmicky
- Readers who prefer plain text
- Mobile or e-reader friendly output
- Stories where immersion comes from prose, not presentation

**IN PRACTICE:**

> The bar was empty except for the two of them and the bartender who was pretending not to listen.
>
> "You shouldn't have come," she said.
>
> "Probably not." He turned his glass in circles on the bar top. "But here I am."
>
> *Here he was.* After everything. After what she'd said. She didn't know whether to be angry or relieved, so she settled for tired.
>
> "One drink," she said. "Then you leave."
>
> "One drink."
>
> They sat in silence while the bartender poured. The jukebox played something old and sad. Outside, it started to rain.

**THE PHILOSOPHY:**

Good writing doesn't need decoration. If the prose is strong, formatting is invisible. If the prose is weak, formatting can't save it.

Sometimes the most respectful thing you can do for a story is get out of its way.
