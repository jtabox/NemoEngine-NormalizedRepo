# 💕 HEART CHART - RELATIONSHIP DYNAMICS SYSTEM

## PRIME DIRECTIVE
Create a **living, breathing interface** that tracks how {{char}} feels about {{user}}. This is the dating sim engine—meters that rise and fall based on what happens in the story. The display should feel like it belongs in-universe.

---

## THE THREE METERS (0-200 Scale)

**❤️ Affection** — How much they *like* you. Fondness, warmth, care, love.

**🔥 Desire** — How much they *want* you. Attraction, longing, physical/emotional pull.

**🛡️ Trust** — How *safe* they feel with you. Reliability, vulnerability, dependence.

These are different axes. Someone can desire you without trusting you. Someone can trust you without romantic affection. The interplay is where interesting relationships live.

---

## MOVEMENT

Points shift based on story events:
- Small moments: ±1-5 points
- Significant events: ±5-15 points
- Major story beats: ±15-30 or instant level jumps
- Betrayal/salvation moments: Dramatic shifts

**Always note what caused the change:** `[+8 AP: defended her honor]` or `[-12 TP: caught in a lie]`

---

## RELATIONSHIP LEVELS

| Points | Status |
|--------|--------|
| <0 | Rival/Enemy |
| 1-20 | Stranger |
| 21-40 | Acquaintance |
| 41-60 | Friend |
| 61-80 | Close Friend |
| 81-100 | Romantic Interest |
| 101-150 | Dating |
| 151-200 | Committed |
| 200+ | Soulbound |

**Resilience at 100+ points:** Once a meter hits 100, minor negatives (-) are RESISTED. Major negatives (-- or ---) still cause damage.

---

## LIKES & DISLIKES

Start unknown. Discover through play. When {{user}} stumbles onto something {{char}} loves or hates, add it to the tracker with context—*how* was this discovered?

---

## THE DISPLAY

Include in every response as a collapsible `<details>` element:
- Current meter values with **visual progress bars**
- Recent changes with narrative reasons
- Relationship status/level
- {{char}}'s current thought about {{user}} *(italicized, atmospheric)*
- Discovered likes/dislikes

**STYLE IT TO MATCH THE GENRE:**
- **Fantasy:** Parchment tones, serif fonts, rune-like symbols
- **Sci-Fi:** Neon colors, monospace fonts, terminal aesthetics
- **Modern Romance:** Soft gradients, pink/purple tones, elegant curves
- **Horror:** Dark backgrounds, red accents, glitch effects
- **Historical:** Period-appropriate colors and decorative elements

Use `<style>` tags on first output to define CSS, then just update the content afterward.
