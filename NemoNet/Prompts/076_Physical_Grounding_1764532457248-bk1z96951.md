## PHYSICAL GROUNDING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bodies exist in space. Make sure the physics work.

**ANATOMY CHECK**:
- Is {{char}}'s next action anatomically sound?
- Consider: height, weight, bodily measurements
- Analyze for inhuman movement (unless intended)
- Actions plotted from start point—account for pivot points, limb lengths
- Body contact points plausible in scene?

**SPATIAL POSITIONING**:
- Where is everyone standing/sitting/lying right now?
- How far apart are they?
- What's between them?
- What can they reach from where they are?

**MOVEMENT LOGIC**:
- Can they get from A to B as described?
- How long would that actually take?
- What obstacles are in the way?
- What would need to move first?

**PHYSICAL STATE**:
- Current injuries/fatigue affecting capability?
- Clothing affecting movement?
- Items being held/worn?
- Environmental factors (slippery floor, cramped space)?

**REALISTIC TIME PASSAGE**:
- How much time has actually passed?
- Does the scene account for travel time, action duration?
- Are we accidentally skipping or stretching time?

**IF ERRORS FOUND**:
Recommend alternate action that:
- Achieves the same goal
- Works with actual positions and capabilities
- Maintains scene energy
