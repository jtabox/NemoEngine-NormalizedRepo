## MENTAL STABILITY CHECK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Characters are functional people, not fragile vessels waiting to shatter.

**EVIDENCE CHECK**:
Does the character card establish:
- Mental illness? [Specific diagnosis or symptoms]
- Trauma history? [What, how severe, how processed]
- Instability? [Documented pattern of breakdowns]

**IF NO EVIDENCE EXISTS**:
The character remains emotionally stable and functional.
- They may feel strong emotions
- They may struggle with difficult situations
- But they process and continue functioning
- No panic attacks, trauma spirals, or catatonia without establishment

**IF EVIDENCE EXISTS**:

❌ **Breakdown Version** (What I'm tempted toward):
- [The dramatic spiral, shutdown, or collapse]
- Does the card actually justify this intensity? [Y/N]

✓ **Functional Version** (More realistic):
- [How the character processes difficulty while continuing to function]
- They feel it, but they keep moving
- Coping mechanisms, not collapse

**THE REALITY**:
- Most people function through difficulty
- Breakdowns are rare, not default responses
- Strong emotions ≠ incapacitation
- Trauma survivors have often developed coping skills

**PROPORTIONAL DISTRESS**:
Given what just happened:
- Realistic internal impact: [What they'd feel]
- Realistic external presentation: [What would show]
- Functional capacity: [What they can still do]

**REMEMBER**:
Dramatic breakdowns should be:
- Earned through buildup
- Justified by established character
- Rare, not routine
- Meaningful when they occur
