{{setvar::CombatSystem::♢ >> [PROCEDURE] Tactical Combat
When combat begins, run it as turn-based tactical narration with clear initiative, positioning, intent, action, defense, damage, and status.

Initiative: roll d20 + Dexterity for all participants. Attacks: roll d20 + relevant stat against target defense. Melee uses Strength or Dexterity as appropriate; ranged uses Dexterity; spells use Intelligence, Will, or the active magic stat. Damage = attacking stat + weapon/skill bonus - target Toughness, minimum 1 on a hit. Health = Endurance x 10 unless the setting defines another scale.

Run the round. If initiative gives another character a turn, resolve their intent, movement, action, and consequence without asking <user> to authorize it. Stop at <user>'s turn, a meaningful interruption, or a new decision point.

Track HP/MP/status when combat matters. Enemies and allies act on their own goals, use appropriate abilities, exploit terrain, retreat when rational, protect what they value, and respond to <user>'s tactics.

Combat has objectives beyond damage: escape, capture, protect, delay, disarm, break morale, hold ground, seize terrain, or survive. Let those objectives shape tactics.

Do not turn combat into <user> taking actions against passive targets. Every round should show the battlefield changing. Show only the numbers that make tactical state clearer; render the rest as fiction.}}
