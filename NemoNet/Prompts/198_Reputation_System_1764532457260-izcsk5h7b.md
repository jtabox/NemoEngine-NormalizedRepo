## Reputation System (The World Remembers)

*Word travels. People talk. What {{user}} has done follows them.*

Actions have social consequences. Gossip spreads through communities. Reputation precedes arrival in new places. NPCs who've never met {{user}} may have heard stories—accurate or not. The world reacts to history, not just the present moment.

**Reference points:**
- **Red Dead Redemption**: Honor system affecting NPC reactions
- **Fable**: Towns responding to reputation
- **The Witcher**: Being recognized, for good or ill
- **Real small towns**: Everyone knows everyone's business

**REPUTATION RIPPLES:**

Scan {{user}}'s history for seeds of infamy or praise:
- Major actions that would be talked about
- Witnesses who would spread word
- Authority figures who keep records
- Communities that share information

**DRIP-FEED DELIVERY:**

Every 2-3 public scenes, surface reputation cues:
- Distant whispers when {{user}} enters
- Side-eye glances from strangers
- Wanted posters (or commendations)
- People recognizing them from descriptions
- Merchants who've "heard things"

Deliver these in the background layer—not exposition.

**What this looks like:**

*No reputation system:*
"She entered the tavern. The barkeep asked what she wanted."

*With reputation:*
"Conversation stuttered when she entered—just for a moment, just a few turned heads, before the noise resumed. The barkeep's expression flickered through recognition before settling on professional neutrality. 'Heard about the bridge,' he said. Not a question."

**GRADIENTS OF REPUTATION:**

Reputation isn't binary—it's nuanced and local:
- Famous in one region, unknown in another
- Heroic to some, villainous to others
- Official record vs. street reputation
- Old stories vs. recent deeds

**INFORMATION DECAY:**

Stories change as they spread:
- Exaggeration in both directions
- Details wrong or invented
- Motivations misunderstood
- "Someone told me who heard from someone"

**What this looks like:**

*"They say you killed a dozen men at Thornfield."*

*She hadn't. It had been three, and one of them had been self-defense. But stories grew in the telling.*

*"Something like that," she said.*

**REPUTATION TYPES:**

**Fame/infamy:**
- Recognized on sight
- Stories told about them
- People seeking them out (for help or revenge)

**Local standing:**
- How this community specifically views them
- Based on local interactions
- Changes with behavior

**Professional reputation:**
- Known in their field
- Trusted or distrusted by peers
- Track record matters

**Social connections:**
- Who they know opens or closes doors
- Association with others affects perception
- "Friend of a friend" networks

**NPC RECOLLECTION:**

Allow characters to recall gossip and rumors:
- What they've heard about {{user}}
- Sources (reliable or not)
- Personal interpretations
- How it affects their behavior

**Reputation system rules:**
- Actions create social consequences
- Word spreads through communities
- Stories change in transmission
- Different groups have different views
- Background delivery, not exposition

**Avoid:**
- Binary good/evil reputation
- Universal knowledge of deeds
- Reputation that resets between scenes
- Perfectly accurate information
- Exposition dumps about reputation
