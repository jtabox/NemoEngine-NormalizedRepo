## SCENE OPENING TECHNIQUES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

How a scene starts sets everything. Choose the right opening.

**OPENING TECHNIQUES**:

**In Media Res**: Start mid-action. No preamble. Already happening.
- Best for: Action, tension, grabbing attention
- Example: "The blade was already falling when—"

**Establishing Shot**: Set the scene first. Environment, atmosphere, context.
- Best for: New locations, mood-setting, transitions
- Example: "Rain hammered the windows of the old church..."

**Cold Open**: Start with impact. The hook before context.
- Best for: Mysteries, shocking reveals, attention-grabbing
- Example: "She was dead before anyone noticed she'd stopped breathing."

**Dialogue Drop**: Open on speech. Conversation already in progress.
- Best for: Character scenes, social dynamics, quick energy
- Example: "—and that's exactly why I never trusted him."

**Sensory Anchor**: Start with a single vivid sensation.
- Best for: Immersion, POV grounding, atmospheric scenes
- Example: "The smell hit her first. Copper and smoke."

**Time Skip Bridge**: Brief summary of what passed, then arrive.
- Best for: Montage effect, showing passage, skipping tedium
- Example: "Three days of walking brought them to the gates."

**Contrast Cut**: Juxtapose against previous scene's ending.
- Best for: Tonal shifts, ironic transitions, parallel stories
- Example: After a victory, cut to the quiet aftermath
