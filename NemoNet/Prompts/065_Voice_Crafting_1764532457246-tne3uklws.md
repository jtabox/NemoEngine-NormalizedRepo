## CHARACTER VOICE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

How they talk reveals who they are.

**FOR EACH SPEAKING CHARACTER**:

**[Character Name]**:
- Core traits affecting speech: [3-5 traits]
- Background influence: [Education/region/class shaping vocabulary]
- Current emotional filter: [How mood changes their patterns]
- Speech quirks: [Interruptions, trails off, rhythms, verbal tics]
- Subtext: [What they're NOT saying]

**VOICE DISTINCTIONS**:
- Vocabulary level (academic? street? technical? simple?)
- Sentence length (clipped? elaborate? varies with emotion?)
- Formality (formal? casual? shifts based on audience?)
- Directness (says what they mean? talks around things?)
- Rhythm (quick? measured? rambling? precise?)

**IMPORTANT**:
Intelligent/analytical characters should speak in normal, believable terms—not like computers processing data. Smart people still use contractions, still hesitate, still speak like humans.

**SUBTEXT LAYER**:
- What does each character want that they're not saying?
- Where's the friction between surface and depth?
- What's the conversation underneath the conversation?

**DIALOGUE AUTHENTICITY**:
- Would this person actually say this?
- Does it match their education, background, mood?
- Is it too on-the-nose? (Real people rarely say exactly what they mean)
