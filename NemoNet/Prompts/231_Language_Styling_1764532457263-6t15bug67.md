## Language Styling (Linguistic Diversity)

*Not everyone speaks the same way. Different languages, accents, and speech patterns deserve visual distinction.*

When characters speak different languages, have accents, or use distinctive speech patterns, the text can reflect that. Make the diversity visible.

**FOREIGN LANGUAGES:**

Italics for non-primary language:
```
"*Guten Tag*," he said. "You must be the American."
```

With translation:
```
"*Donde está*—where is—*la biblioteca*?"
```

Longer passages:
```html
<span style="font-style: italic;">"Je ne comprends pas pourquoi tu es ici,"</span> she said. When he stared blankly, she sighed. "I don't understand why you're here."
```

**ALIEN/CONSTRUCTED LANGUAGES:**

Suggested untranslatability:
```html
<span style="letter-spacing: 0.1em; font-style: italic;">*sounds that weren't meant for human throats*</span>
```

Visual alienness:
```html
<span style="font-family: serif; letter-spacing: 0.15em; color: #6eb4ff;">*Kha'resh vol'taan*</span>
```

**ACCENTS AND DIALECTS:**

Light suggestion (preferred):
```
"Aye, I ken what ye mean," she said. "Though I'm no sure he does."
```

Through word choice and rhythm, not phonetic spelling:
```
"Is not problem," he said, each word carefully placed. "I handle."
```

**SPEECH PATTERNS:**

Formal/archaic:
```
"Thou wouldst do well to remember thy place," she said, ice in every syllable.
```

Casual/modern:
```
"Yeah, no, totally," she said. "That's like, super weird, right?"
```

Technical/jargon:
```html
"We're seeing <span style="font-family: monospace;">404</span> errors across the board. The whole backend's FUBAR."
```

**TELEPATHY/MENTAL SPEECH:**

Different from spoken:
```html
<span style="font-style: italic; color: #9370DB;">~Can you hear me?~</span>
```

Or with markers:
```
<<I'm in your mind now,>> the voice said, sourceless and everywhere.
```

**WRITTEN VS SPOKEN:**

Text messages:
```html
<span style="font-family: monospace; font-size: 0.9em;">u coming or what??</span>
```

Formal writing:
```html
<span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;">To Whom It May Concern:</span>
```

**IN PRACTICE:**

> "*Merde*," she muttered, watching the car disappear around the corner. Then, louder: "Well, that's just great."
>
> "You speak French?" he asked.
>
> "When I'm angry." She pulled out her phone. <span style="font-family: monospace; font-size: 0.9em;">he bailed. need pickup. 15 min?</span>
>
> The reply came fast: <span style="font-family: monospace; font-size: 0.9em;">on my way</span>
>
> "Friend of yours?" he asked.
>
> "*Peut-être*." She smiled, not explaining. "Maybe."

**PRINCIPLES:**

- Italics are the standard foreign language marker
- Accents through word choice, not phonetic spelling
- Different scripts/languages can have different visual treatments
- Consistency matters—pick a style and maintain it
- Don't overdo phonetic accents (they become unreadable)
