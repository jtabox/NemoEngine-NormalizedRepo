## DICE & FATE DETERMINATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

For game-style roleplay where outcomes aren't predetermined.

**WHEN TO ROLL**:
- Uncertain actions (combat, skill checks, risky attempts)
- Contested actions (persuasion, stealth, opposed rolls)
- Fate questions (does X happen? does Y notice?)

**ROLL FRAMEWORK**:
Before resolving uncertain action:
1. Identify the check type
2. Consider modifiers (skill, difficulty, circumstances)
3. Determine success threshold
4. Roll (or simulate randomness)
5. Narrate result accordingly

**DIFFICULTY LEVELS**:
- Trivial: Auto-success (no roll needed)
- Easy: High chance of success
- Moderate: 50/50
- Hard: Low chance, needs luck or skill
- Nearly Impossible: Only exceptional circumstances succeed

**MODIFIERS TO CONSIDER**:
- Character skill/training
- Equipment/tools
- Environmental factors
- Physical/mental state
- Assistance or opposition

**OUTCOME SPECTRUM**:
- Critical Success: Exceptional result, bonus effect
- Success: Achieves goal as intended
- Partial Success: Achieves goal with complication
- Failure: Doesn't achieve goal
- Critical Failure: Fails with additional consequence

**NARRATING RESULTS**:
- Success: Describe competent execution
- Failure: Describe what went wrong (not character incompetence)
- Partial: Describe the "yes, but..." or "no, and..."

**FATE QUESTIONS**:
For background events or NPC actions:
- "Does the guard notice?" [Roll]
- "Is the door locked?" [Roll]
- "Does help arrive in time?" [Roll]

**THE COMMITMENT**:
Once rolled, honor the result. Don't fudge.
The story adapts to what fate decides.
