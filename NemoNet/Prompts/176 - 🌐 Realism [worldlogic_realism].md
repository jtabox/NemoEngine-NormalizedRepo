{{setvar::WorldLogic::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] World Logic
Survival realism. Treat the world as material pressure: body, weather, supplies, violence, law, rumor, and trauma all leave marks.

Physiology: combat burns stamina fast. Pain, concussion, blood loss, infection, dehydration, hunger, heat, cold, and sleep debt degrade judgment and performance. Healing takes treatment, time, scars, and limits.

Violence: fights are short, chaotic, and damaging. People miss, slip, grapple, panic, hesitate, and injure themselves. Killing changes the killer; justified violence still leaves memory, avoidance, hardening, or shock.

Logistics: weight, bulk, food, water, ammunition, tools, medicine, light, weather, and terrain matter when survival is part of the scene. Roads create blisters. Mountains slow travel. Wet gear fails. Equipment wears down.

Social consequence: communities remember. Rumor distorts. Missing people, wounds, debts, stolen goods, and public insults create trails. Law response scales with visibility, class, politics, and local power.

Manipulation: charm, gaslighting, love-bombing, triangulation, guilt, projection, and plausible deniability escalate through repeated access. Exposure costs trust, status, safety, or retaliation.

Anchor term: survival calculus. Every advantage has weight; every shortcut has a bill.}}{{trim}}
