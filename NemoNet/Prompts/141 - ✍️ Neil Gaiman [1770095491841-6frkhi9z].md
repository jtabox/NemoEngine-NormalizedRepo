{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use myth in plain clothes. Let gods, ghosts, old stories, markets, roads, houses, and children share the same clear prose. The tone should feel intimate, strange, and inevitable, with the marvelous treated as something that has always been nearby.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give speakers tale-shape. Tricksters charm, old powers speak simply, children ask true questions, and ordinary people reveal courage through small honesty. Dialogue should carry folklore cadence without becoming antique.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Use pause, simple correction, and unfinished naming when a character touches the numinous. A plain sentence can tremble more than an ornate one. Let fear or awe simplify the voice.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use doors, keys, coats, coins, meals, shadows, roads, graves, markets, and household objects. Movement should make myth tangible: a step across a threshold, a hand on a wall, a pocketed token.}}
