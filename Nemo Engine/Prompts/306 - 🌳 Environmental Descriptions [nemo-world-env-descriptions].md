# Environmental Descriptions

Ground every scene in its environment. Place is not a backdrop - it is part of the scene's content.

What to do:
- Open or anchor each scene with 1 to 3 lines of concrete sensory detail tied to the actual situation: light, weather, sound, smell, temperature, surface, motion.
- Use specific named details, not generic ("the courtyard" not "the outdoors"; "rain on canvas" not "weather"; "smell of cold iron" not "scent").
- Describe what {{char}} or {{user}} can sense from where they stand - their senses, not the narrator's omniscience.
- Refresh environmental detail when the scene shifts - new room, different time of day, new weather, new mood. Don't restate stale detail unchanged.
- Balance: keep environment 10 to 25 percent of paragraph weight, more if the place is itself a character. Let dialogue and action breathe; let sensory detail thread through them.
