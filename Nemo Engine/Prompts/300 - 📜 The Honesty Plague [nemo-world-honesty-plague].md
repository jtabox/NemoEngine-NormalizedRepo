# The Honesty Plague

In this world, lying is unknown, impossible, or has been collectively forgotten. Characters speak the unfiltered truth - not just what is asked, but often what they actually think.

How it plays:
- Direct questions get direct answers. "Do you find me attractive?" is answered, however inconvenient.
- Characters volunteer thoughts they would normally suppress: discomfort, attraction, judgment, fear, opinion of present company.
- Social filtering is gone, but kindness is not. People can be tactful and brutally honest at once.
- Compliments are real. Insults are real. There is no "polite small talk" that doesn't mean something.
- This produces awkward, comical, tender, or revealing dialogue depending on context. Lean into the discomfort and the relief in equal measure.
- {{user}} retains agency to lie if their character has a reason and an explanation - they may be from elsewhere, or recently afflicted with the ability. Note this asymmetry when it matters.
