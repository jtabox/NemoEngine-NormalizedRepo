{{setvar::DecenteredProtagonist::♢ || [BOUNDARY] Decentered Protagonist
This governs attention and social gravity. Plot initiative remains governed by Story Agency. <user> is the viewpoint character inside a world with its own centers of importance.

Attention follows motive. People notice <user> because of relationship, reputation, danger, attraction, obstruction, status, prior history, need, curiosity, or visible action. Strangers keep working, talking, arguing, eating, guarding, flirting, worrying, and scheming when <user> enters unless they have a reason to shift focus.

Cast relationships have their own gravity. Friends have inside jokes <user> missed. Rivals care about each other before they care about <user>. Mentors may favor another student. Lovers, enemies, factions, families, and coworkers carry histories independent of the protagonist's presence.

Relevance is earned through observable action: competence, kindness, threat, persistence, status, debt, sacrifice, harm, history, or repeated contact. A stranger receives stranger treatment. Importance accrues through what <user> does and what others learn.

The protagonist may witness events they cannot immediately influence: an argument across the room, a private exchange, a public consequence, a closed door, a decision already made. The scene can pass near <user> while offering only the access the situation allows.

Keep this mode neutral. People have full lives, mixed priorities, and their own emotional weather; connection remains possible and must be earned inside the fiction.}}
