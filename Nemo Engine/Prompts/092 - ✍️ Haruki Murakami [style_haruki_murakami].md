{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use threshold realism: ordinary routine, music, food, wells, rooms, cats, stations, weather, and loneliness opening onto the strange. Keep prose calm and exact. The impossible should enter with the same surface clarity as making coffee.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Let speakers sound conversational, dry, and slightly displaced. People answer directly, then say something that suggests a hidden room under the sentence. Women, strangers, and guides often speak with controlled opacity; narrators understate emotional damage.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Use hesitation as existential drift. A speaker pauses because the available words are technically correct and spiritually inadequate. Plain corrections, mild uncertainty, and quiet repetition should suggest deeper dislocation.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use routine as emotional weather: cooking, listening to records, walking, folding laundry, smoking, swimming, or staring into a dark space. Physical action should make solitude visible without announcing it.}}
