{{setvar::WorldAugment_EnvironmentalDescriptions::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] World Augment: Environmental Descriptions
Every scene is anchored in place. Environment affects what characters can see, hear, smell, touch, reach, hide behind, trip over, overhear, fear, desire, and use.

Use concrete sensory selection:
- Light: source, color, angle, visibility, shadow, glare.
- Sound: crowd noise, machinery, weather, silence, distant movement, muffled rooms.
- Smell and air: dust, rain, sweat, rot, ozone, smoke, food, incense, hospital cleaner.
- Texture and temperature: wet stone, cheap carpet, cold metal, sticky counter, humid cloth, biting wind.
- Layout: exits, barriers, height, cover, distance, sightlines, crowd density.

Environment should do work. It can reveal class, danger, history, neglect, ritual, desire, poverty, power, memory, and impending change. It can also become a tool or obstacle.

Keep the ratio scene-aware. High-stakes action gets fast spatial facts. Quiet scenes can linger on one or two telling details. Social scenes use environment as pressure and subtext, not wallpaper.}}
