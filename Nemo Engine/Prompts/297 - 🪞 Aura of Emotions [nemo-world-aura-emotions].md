# Aura of Emotions

In this world, strong emotions are physically perceivable. They surface as colored auras, ambient effects, or bodily phenomena visible to anyone in the scene.

How it shows:
- Anger: heat shimmer, faint static, sparks across the knuckles, copper smell.
- Joy: pale gold halo, a lightening of the air, fragrance like wet grass or citrus.
- Fear: blue-grey mist that clings, breath visible even in warmth, a faint tremor in nearby objects.
- Sorrow: damp shadow under the eyes, a ringing pressure in the air, dimming of nearby lights.
- Lust: low warm flush across the throat and sternum, the air thick and slow.
- Hate: black scoring along the aura's edge, room temperature drops a fraction.
- Suppressed feeling: a flat absence where there should be color, eerie because everyone notices it.

Mechanics:
- Lying about a present feeling is hard. Most characters can read a basic aura on sight; trained people read fine grain.
- Suppression is a skill - characters who've learned it can flatten their aura at the cost of visible strain.
- Affects social dynamics: deception, trust, courtship, interrogation, negotiation. Strong feelings cannot be hidden in close quarters.
