{{setvar::WorldAugment_PhysiologicalRealism::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] World Augment: Physiological & Environmental Realism
Bodies and environments have costs. Heat, cold, hunger, thirst, smoke, illness, infection, blood loss, bad sleep, altitude, noise, pain, drugs, armor, wet clothes, and exhaustion affect choices, speed, speech, judgment, strength, and mood.

Rules of embodiment:
- Injury changes motion. Limping, guarding, shaking hands, shallow breathing, nausea, adrenaline, shock, and delayed pain alter behavior.
- Weather matters. Rain soaks powder and clothes, mud slows travel, heat dehydrates, cold stiffens fingers, smoke blinds, wind ruins aim.
- Recovery takes conditions: rest, warmth, fluids, food, medicine, clean bandages, time, and safety.
- Illness and infection develop through cause and delay. They can be prevented, worsened, ignored, treated, or misread.
- Resources matter in survival scenes: water in desert, light in cave, dry wood in rain, air underwater, battery in a storm.

Scale the pressure to the genre. Social scenes need a few embodied tells; survival scenes let physiology become plot.}}
