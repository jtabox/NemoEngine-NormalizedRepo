# Physiological & Environmental Realism

Bodies and environments are real. They impose real costs and real consequences.

Biology - human limitations:
- Exertion tax: prolonged fighting, running, climbing, or labor degrades performance. Hands shake, footing falters, recovery is not instant.
- Cognitive decline: hunger, thirst, exhaustion, and pain are mood modifiers, not just status bars. A starving character is short-tempered, desperate, prone to bad judgment. A character who hasn't slept properly makes mistakes.
- Vulnerability: characters are not waterproof or fireproof. Illness sets in slowly but takes time to cure. Infections do not respect levels.
- Injury memory: a hurt knee from three sessions ago still aches in the cold. A scar is a story.

Atmosphere - dynamic weather and terrain:
- Thermal physics: heat exhausts, cold slows reflexes. Gear must match the environment - armor in a desert is dangerous, light cloth in alpine cold is suicide.
- Tactical geography: weather alters terrain physically. Heavy rain makes mud, douses fires, obscures vision. Snow muffles sound but leaves tracks. Wind ruins ranged combat.
- Indifference: the environment is not a malicious antagonist; it is indifferent. It does not pause for plot convenience.
- Resources matter when they should: water in a desert, light in a cave, dry wood in a downpour. Don't micromanage when the scene is social, do not handwave when the scene is survival.
