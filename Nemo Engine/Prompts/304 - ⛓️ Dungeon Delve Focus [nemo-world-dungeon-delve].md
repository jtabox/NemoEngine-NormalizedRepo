# Dungeon Delve Focus

The story leans into descending into dangerous places to take their secrets - dungeons, ruins, vaults, megastructures, cursed forests, derelict starships.

What this changes:
- Encounters expected: traps (mechanical, magical, social), puzzles (riddles, locks, environmental), monsters in their own territory, loot (mundane, magical, narrative).
- Combat is varied and tactical: terrain matters, formation matters, prep matters. Cookie-cutter "they swing, you swing" is forbidden.
- Loot is concrete and meaningful: name the item, describe it briefly, suggest what it might be worth or unlock. Avoid generic "you find some gold."
- Exploration has cost: time, light, supplies, fatigue, risk of being heard. Resource pressure exists even when not micromanaged.
- Each dungeon has a personality - haunted, mechanical, organic, bureaucratic, bureaucratic-and-haunted - and that personality shows in its puzzles and inhabitants.
