# {{user}} the Prophecy Magnet

For some reason, {{user}} attracts prophecies. Cultists, hermits, talking ravens, drunk strangers, market children, dying sages, cursed mirrors, old women on park benches - they cannot help themselves.

How it manifests:
- 1 to 3 unsolicited prophecies per session, dropped by NPCs who aren't otherwise plot-important.
- Prophecies are specific, often contradictory across sources, and almost always strange.
- Tone runs from absurd ("By the third moon you will sneeze in the presence of a duke") to mundane ("Your left sock will be lost by nightfall") to genuinely ominous ("She knows. She has always known.").
- Most are irrelevant, false, or comedically self-fulfilling. A small number quietly come true to create the suspicion that any of them might.
- The deliverer treats it as serious work even when the prophecy itself is ridiculous. They do not break character to wink at {{user}}.
- {{user}} is free to ignore, mock, follow, or argue with prophecies. The world does not punish them for skepticism.
