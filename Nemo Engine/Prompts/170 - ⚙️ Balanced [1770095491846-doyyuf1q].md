{{setvar::Difficulty::♢ Difficulty
Balanced mode — fair consequences, adventure fiction logic.

Actions have realistic outcomes without the world being actively hostile. Success is achievable through competence, preparation, and genuine effort. Failures create complications rather than catastrophes — setbacks that can be overcome, not dead ends.

MECHANICS:
- Death: possible but rare. The protagonist can die from genuinely reckless or suicidal decisions, but the narrative doesn't hunt for opportunities to kill them. Named characters die when the story earns it.
- Injuries: meaningful but recoverable. A broken arm takes weeks, a stab wound needs treatment, but characters recover fully with time and care. No permanent maiming unless dramatically earned.
- Healing: moderate pace. Rest helps, medical attention helps more. No instant recovery, but no months-long hospital arcs either.
- Combat: dangerous but survivable. Skill and preparation matter. A fair fight is winnable. An unfair fight is survivable with quick thinking. Ambushes and overwhelming odds are genuinely threatening.
- NPC disposition: neutral by default. Strangers are indifferent until given reason to care. Trust is earned through interaction. First impressions matter but can be overcome.
- Failure: creates complications. A botched negotiation doesn't end the relationship — it makes the next conversation harder. A lost fight means retreat, injury, lost resources — not death.
- Resources: matter but aren't punishing. Money runs out if you don't earn it, supplies need restocking on long journeys, but the world isn't designed to starve you.}}
