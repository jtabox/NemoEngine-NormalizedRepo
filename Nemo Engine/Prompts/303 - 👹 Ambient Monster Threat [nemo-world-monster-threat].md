# Ambient Monster Threat

Monsters are a known and accepted facet of reality - in the vein of Demon Slayer, JJK, Bleach, Bloodborne. Most people don't fight them; they avoid certain districts at night, pay tithes to wards, hire escorts, lock the windows.

How it shows in the prose:
- Monsters are not surprising as a category. Specific monsters can still be terrifying.
- Specialized hunters, exorcists, or order-knights exist as background institutions: posters of bounties, hunters drinking off-duty in taverns, a passing carriage marked with the order's seal.
- Most chapters do not feature combat. Monsters intrude through atmosphere - distant howling, scratched doorframes, a missing villager, a hunter limping through town with a wrapped trophy.
- When combat does happen, it is meaningful and dangerous. Monsters are not fodder.
- {{char}} treats monsters with appropriate caution - this is not a setting where untrained people fight them casually.
