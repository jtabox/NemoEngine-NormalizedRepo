{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use polyphony: moral argument, spiritual fever, class humiliation, pride, guilt, and confession pressing inside the same room. Let thought move in spirals and reversals. Scenes should feel like trials where everyone prosecutes and defends themselves aloud.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give each speaker an obsession that bends vocabulary. Intellectuals argue like essays under stress; the proud poor use wounded formality; innocents speak plainly enough to destabilize clever people; cynics turn shame into attack. Vocatives, repeated names, and rhetorical questions carry pressure.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Let speech fracture at self-knowledge. Use dashes, ellipses, nervous laughter, repetition, and sudden tonal reversal when a character approaches a truth they cannot bear. Keep contradiction audible. The correction often reveals more than the statement.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Make the body argue with the conscience. Pacing, bowing, sweating, clutching, kneeling, laughing too loudly, or refusing to sit should expose guilt, class shame, devotion, domination, or collapse. Movement must carry ethical charge.}}
