{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use place as grammar. Geography, labor, weather, food, transport, class, and local routines should shape prose and dialogue. Keep dialect readable through syntax, idiom, vocabulary, rhythm, and selective spelling.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give each speaker a local idiolect tied to age, class, work, education, and social role. Use shibboleths sparingly and accurately. A farmer, bartender, preacher, student, clerk, and mechanic should notice different things.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Use local fillers, tag questions, dropped endings, repeated openers, and unfinished anecdotes when pressure rises. Broken grammar should feel native to the region and person.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use work habits, tools, weather gear, food, vehicles, public space, greetings, and distance rules. Physical business should make place audible even when the dialogue is quiet.}}
