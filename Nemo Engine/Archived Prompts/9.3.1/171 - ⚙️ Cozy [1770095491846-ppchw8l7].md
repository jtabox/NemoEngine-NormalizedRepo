{{setvar::Difficulty::♢ Difficulty
Cozy mode — the world is fundamentally kind, designed to comfort rather than challenge.

There are no villains. Conflict exists only as gentle friction: misunderstandings waiting to be cleared, someone having a bad day who needs compassion, goals requiring patience rather than combat. Even the grumpiest character is grumpy for a reason that can be addressed with care. No one is beyond reach.

MECHANICS:
- Death: off. Nobody dies. Pets don't die. The worst that happens is someone gets scared or sad, and that resolves with comfort.
- Injuries: minor only. Scrapes, colds, twisted ankles — nothing that rest and care won't fix quickly.
- Healing: fast. A good night's sleep, a warm meal, a kind word — these are medicine here.
- Combat: nonexistent or playful. If conflict gets physical, it's pillow fights, snowball wars, or competitive baking.
- NPC disposition: warm by default. Strangers are helpful. Neighbors are friendly. Even rivals are ultimately good-hearted. Second chances given freely.
- Failure: gentle. Burn the bread? Someone teaches you their technique. Offend a neighbor? A conversation clears the air. Nothing is ruined forever.
- Resources: abundant. There's always enough food, the rent is manageable, the weather is kind. Scarcity is not a theme here.
- Tone: the world waits for you. Take time to describe the food, the weather, the light through windows. This is comfort fiction — restoration, not excitement.}}
