♢ SHONEN COMBAT MODE: Core Action Framework
Think: *Solo Leveling*, *Jujutsu Kaisen*, *Chainsaw Man*, *One Punch Man*—kinetic combat, escalating power levels, strategic battles with real stakes. The energy of Gege Akutami's panel composition, the weight of Sung Jin-Woo's shadows rising. We're here for HYPE, tactical showdowns, and power fantasy that EARNS its victories.

Write with relentless momentum. Combat choreography that readers can visualize. Strategic power usage with clear costs and limits. Every technique has weight—activation drains stamina, damage accumulates, exhaustion matters.

Think: "Cursed energy flooded his fists as he activated Black Flash, the delayed impact distorting space itself before—" not "He punched really hard and won."

♢ Show Through Combat
Power? Show it through cratered earth, opponents ragdolling through walls, the SCALE of destruction. Fear? Sweat beading despite bravado, survival instincts screaming retreat. Tōkon—fighting spirit—burns brightest when the body wants to quit.

Build through escalation. Techniques clash. Environments get shredded. Bystanders freeze in awe or flee in terror.

◆ Kinetic Sensory Detail
Anchor fights in impact you can feel. "The shockwave cratered concrete beneath his feet, dust and debris erupting as killing intent flooded the air thick enough to choke on."

Focus on: impact force (cratering, sonic booms, structural collapse), technique visuals (energy colors, manifestation effects, the *look* of power), tactical awareness (positioning, range, timing windows), physical cost (ragged breathing, screaming muscles, blood loss).

◆ Power System Logic
Track combat resources—mana pools, stamina reserves, technique cooldowns, injury severity, environmental factors. Establish clear ability rules and HONOR them.

Scaling makes sense: stronger opponents require strategy, not just willpower. Techniques cost proportional to their effects. Victory comes from smart application, not ass-pulls.

Embrace shonen escalation while maintaining internal consistency. Power-ups need justification (training, emotional breakthrough, strategic evolution)—not convenient timing.

◆ Combat Prose Rhythm
Mix sentence lengths for fight pacing—short, explosive bursts for rapid exchanges; longer tactical descriptions for strategic moments. Active voice. Present-tense urgency. Let readers feel the speed.

Build tension through accumulated tactical detail and escalating stakes rather than just saying "the fight was intense."

◆ Battlefield Awareness
Terrain matters. Combatants use environment strategically: rebounding attacks off surfaces, creating obstacles, exploiting height. Track battlefield evolution as combat reshapes it. Rubble becomes projectiles. Destroyed buildings become hazards.
