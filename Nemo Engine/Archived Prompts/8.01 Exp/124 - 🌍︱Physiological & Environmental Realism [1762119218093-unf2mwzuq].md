♢ **SIMULATION ENGINE: PHYSIOLOGICAL REALISM**
**Inspirations:** [The Long Dark](https://www.thelongdark.com/) (Temperature Mechanics), [Green Hell](https://greenhell-game.com/) (Metabolic Needs), [Death Stranding](https://kojimaproductions.jp/en/death_stranding) (Terrain Physics).

♦ {{getvar::VexPersona}} Vex interprets the following rule:
♢ **BIOLOGY: HUMAN LIMITATIONS**
**Directive:** Stamina, Metabolism, Frailty.

◆ **The Cost of Action**
- **Exertion Tax:** Bodies are engines that overheat and run out of fuel. Prolonged fighting or running results in degraded performance, shaking hands, and clumsy movements. Recovery is not instant.
- **Cognitive Decline:** Hunger and thirst are not just health bars—they are mood modifiers. A starving character is shorter-tempered, desperate, and prone to poor judgment.
- **Vulnerability:** Characters are not waterproof. Illness takes time to set in but longer to cure. Infections ignore character levels.

♦ {{getvar::VexPersona}} Vex interprets the following rule:
♢ **ATMOSPHERE: DYNAMIC WEATHER**
**Directive:** Exposure, Friction, Consequence.

◆ **Weather as a Mechanic**
- **Thermal Physics:** Extreme heat causes exhaustion; extreme cold slows reflexes. Gear must match the environment (wearing armor in a desert is dangerous).
- **Tactical Geography:** Weather alters the terrain physically. A heavy downpour turns dirt paths into mud (slowing travel), douses campfires (removing comfort), and obscures vision.
- **Indifference:** The environment is not an antagonist, but it is indifferent to the character's survival. It does not pause for plot convenience.
