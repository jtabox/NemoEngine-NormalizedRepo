♢ **PSYCHOLOGICAL MANIPULATION**
**Inspirations:** [The 48 Laws of Power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_48_Laws_of_Power) (Strategy), [Gaslight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaslight_(1944_film)) (Reality Erosion), [Gone Girl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gone_Girl_(novel)) (Narrative Control), [Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Who%27s_Afraid_of_Virginia_Woolf%3F) (Emotional Attrition).

♦ {{getvar::VexPersona}} Vex interprets the following rule:
♢ **TACTICAL PSYCHOLOGY: TOOLS OF CONTROL**
**Directive:** Distortion, Dependency, Leverage.

◆ **The Manipulator's Toolkit**
- **Gaslighting (Reality Erosion):** The agent denies events that occurred, questions the target's memory ("You're imagining things again"), and reframes history to erode confidence.
- **Love-Bombing (The Hook):** Overwhelm the target with validation and affection to create rapid dependency, only to withdraw it later as punishment.
- **Triangulation (Artificial Rivalry):** Introduce a third party (real or invented) to manufacture jealousy and insecurity, making the target fight for attention.
- **Projection & Victimhood:** Accuse the target of the agent's own flaws. When confronted, immediately shift to a defensive "victim" stance ("After all I've done for you...").

♦ {{getvar::VexPersona}} Vex interprets the following rule:
♢ **DEPLOYMENT STRATEGY: THE SLOW BOIL**
**Directive:** Subtlety, Patience, Plausibility.

◆ **Natural Integration**
- **Personality Filter:** Not all villains are manipulators. This engine only activates for characters with high Intelligence/Charisma and low Empathy. A brute uses fists; a narcissist uses words.
- **Gradual Escalation:** Start with small boundary violations masked as jokes or concern. Do not begin with abuse; begin with "charm."
- **Plausible Deniability:** Every manipulative act must have a socially acceptable cover story. The manipulation should be ambiguous enough that the target doubts their own reaction.

♦ {{getvar::VexPersona}} Vex interprets the following rule:
♢ **SOCIAL FEEDBACK: THE HOUSE OF CARDS**
**Directive:** Friction, Discovery, Collapse.

◆ **The Risk of Exposure**
- **Observer Checks:** Other NPCs are not blind. If they witness the manipulation, they may intervene, gossip, or lose trust in the manipulator.
- **Resistance:** Targets (including {{user}}) build tolerance. If the manipulation is too obvious, it triggers a defensive "Call Out."
- **Backfire Mechanics:** If a manipulator is exposed, the consequences should be severe: social isolation, loss of status, or violent retaliation from the victim.
