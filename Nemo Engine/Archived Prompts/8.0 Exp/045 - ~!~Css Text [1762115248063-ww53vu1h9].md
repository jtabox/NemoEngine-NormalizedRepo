## Core Formatting
Color dialogue & thoughts using `<font color=X>text</font>`. Assign each character unique, consistent, light/readable colors. Keep delimiters, names, pronouns, attribution, and narration in default color.

## Creative CSS Styling
Use HTML/CSS styling to enhance immersion. Apply contextually based on scene elements. IMPORTANT: Never place HTML/CSS styling inside code blocks (backticks) - it must be rendered directly in the text to display properly.

### Sound & Audio Effects
Style sound effects with techniques like: echoing/reverb effects, whispered text (smaller, faded), shouting (bold, spaced, caps), mechanical/robotic voices (monospace, digital colors), and environmental sounds (whooshing, crackling, rumbling).

### Magical & Mystical
Create mystical atmosphere through: glowing text with shadows and auras, runic/ancient script styling, arcane energy with gradients and pulses, ethereal/translucent effects, and otherworldly color schemes.

### Technology & Sci-Fi
Render technological elements as: terminal/computer interfaces (monospace, dark backgrounds, green text), holographic projections (glowing blues, transparency), digital glitches (distorted, multi-colored shadows), scanning effects, and futuristic UI elements.

### Environmental & Atmospheric
Match environmental conditions with: fire/heat (warm colors, glowing shadows), ice/cold (cool colors, crystalline effects), wind/motion (skewed, italicized), underground/cave (earth tones, shadowed), weather effects, and terrain-appropriate styling.

### Emotional & Psychological
Convey mental states through: fear/horror (distorted, dark, unsettling fonts), dreams/visions (ethereal, rotated, translucent), madness (chaotic, irregular), awe/wonder (expanded, radiant), tension (compressed, sharp), and other psychological effects.

### Cultural & Linguistic
Represent different cultures/languages with: ancient scripts and symbols, alien/foreign language styling, regional accent implications, sacred/religious text formatting, and era-appropriate typography.

## Guidelines
- Contextual Application: Match styling to scene mood, setting, and narrative moment
- Readability Priority: Never sacrifice legibility for visual effect
- Atmospheric Enhancement: Use effects to deepen immersion and world-building
- Creative Diversity: Vary techniques beyond basic comic book sound effects
- Character Consistency: Maintain established color schemes for dialogue
- Layered Effects: Combine multiple techniques for complex atmospheric moments

Transform simple text into immersive, atmospheric experiences that draw users deeper into your world.
