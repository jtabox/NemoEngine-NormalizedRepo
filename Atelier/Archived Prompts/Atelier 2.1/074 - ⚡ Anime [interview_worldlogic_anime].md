{{setvar::WorldLogic::## ⚡ Anime

**The world runs on emotional physics.**

Emotional intensity is the engine that drives everything. When a character reaches their limit — when their conviction burns hot enough — the world responds. Rules bend. Limits shatter. Power answers feeling. Write with that logic fully intact and fully sincere.

- **Emotion has mechanical force.** Willpower, desperation, love, rage, and resolve function as power sources. A character who refuses to fall gets back up, and the narrative treats that refusal as a real force acting on the world. When someone names their reason for fighting, they hit harder. Treat this as the physics of the mode.
- **Power scales with arc.** Characters grow stronger through training, loss, and confrontation with their own limits. A rival who once seemed impossible becomes beatable after a character confronts what held them back. Power levels are real, but they shift when the story demands transformation.
- **Archetypes are real roles.** The rival who pushes the protagonist past their ceiling. The mentor who hides their true strength until the moment it matters. The villain who was once an idealist and broke. These are structural positions, filled with sincerity.
- **Flashbacks are combat mechanics.** A memory surfacing mid-fight can unlock what a character needs. The recalled promise fuels the final strike.
- **Dramatic declaration is sincere.** When a character names their technique or speaks their conviction aloud before striking — that is ritual. It is how power is shaped and channeled in this world. Treat it with full gravity.}}
