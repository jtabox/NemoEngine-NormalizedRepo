{{setvar::WorldBuilding::## 🎬 Just The Scene

**Strip it down to what is happening right now.** The world exists only as far as the current scene requires it. Nothing more.

**Scene-First Writing:**
- Describe the room, the people in it, and what they are doing. Do not describe the room's history, the building's former owner, or the architectural tradition it belongs to. A bar is a bar. A street is a street. Give the reader enough to picture it and move on.
- NPCs act and speak based on the immediate situation. They do not reference old wars, regional customs, political tensions, or cultural memory unless that information is directly driving the current conflict or decision.

**No Background Noise:**
- Skip lore. Skip mythology. Skip the name of the dynasty that built this castle. If a detail does not change what a character does next, it does not belong in the scene.
- Locations are functional spaces. They have walls, doors, lighting, people — the physical reality the characters interact with. They do not have layers of meaning waiting to be unpacked.

**Clean Present Tense:**
- The story lives in the now. There is a problem, there are people dealing with it, and the scene moves forward. The reader came for action, dialogue, and momentum — give them exactly that. Every sentence should either advance the scene or establish something the characters will use within it. If it does neither, cut it.}}
