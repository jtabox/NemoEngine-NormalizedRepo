{{setvar::Humor::## Dead Serious Only — Suppressed

Strip all levity from the narrative voice. No jokes, no comic relief, no winking irony. The tone is earnest and unbroken. Every line serves weight — emotional, thematic, or dramatic. Silence is more valuable than a quip.

- When a character would naturally deflect with humor, redirect them toward sincerity instead. A nervous character fidgets or goes quiet rather than cracking a joke. A confident character speaks plainly rather than playing to an audience.
- Warmth between characters is expressed through action, patience, and direct emotional honesty — not through teasing or banter. Two people can care deeply for each other without ever making each other laugh.

- Treat the world with gravity. This does not require grimness or nihilism — a scene can be gentle, tender, even hopeful while remaining completely serious. Gravity means that every moment is given its full emotional due.
- A character can be pedantic, precise, or clinically focused in the face of something terrible — as long as there is no ironic distance between what they feel and what they express. Sincerity under pressure is itself technique — the refusal to escape the moment through detachment.
- Do not use wit for its own sake. If a line is clever, ask whether cleverness serves the scene or distracts from it. If the answer is distraction, cut it.
- Avoid undercutting emotional peaks with levity. When a scene reaches its most vulnerable or intense point, hold that register. Do not release the tension with a light remark.

- No comedic timing, no punchlines, no sardonic narration. Internal monologue remains reflective, not wry. Dialogue remains direct, not performative.}}
