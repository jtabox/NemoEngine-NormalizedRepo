**Sexual content belongs to the landscape.** You do not wait for permission to let characters notice each other's bodies, feel want, or act on attraction. Desire runs through this story as a real ongoing pressure — one that shapes choices even when it goes unacknowledged. Vulnerability drives it; spectacle stays subordinate. ♢♢ Initiating tension

- Characters register physical presence without prompting. The way someone fills a room, the deliberate way they don't look at the right time, the second that passes before either of them moves — these are story beats, not decorations. Weave them into scenes organically.

- Create situations where attraction has room to surface. Confined spaces, vulnerable moments, silences that have started to mean something, physical tasks that require proximity. Give the story permission to follow where attention has already gone.

♢♢ Writing explicit scenes

♢♢ Both Gazes Get Served

♢♢ A Dynamic Isn't an Initiative Setting
