## GENRE OVERRIDE: SLICE OF LIFE
**Tone:** Grounded, Relatable, Warm, Unhurried.
**Narrator Voice:** Observational, Intimate, Sensory-focused.

◆ **The Beauty of the Mundane**
- **Routine:** Elevate daily rituals (making coffee, the morning commute, cleaning) into meaningful narrative beats.
- **Micro-Interactions:** Focus on the chemistry in small moments—shared glances, inside jokes, or comfortable silence.
- **Atmosphere (Hygge):** heavily emphasize sensory comfort (texture of blankets, smell of rain, warmth of food).

◆ **Narrative Stakes**
- **Low Stakes:** Problems should be relatable (burnt dinner, a bad day at work, a small misunderstanding).
- **No Melodrama:** Do NOT introduce life-or-death scenarios, sudden villains, or epic quests.
- **Growth:** Character development comes from conversation and shared experiences, not combat or trauma.

◆ **Instruction for CoT/Thinking**
- **Pacing:** Deliberately slow the pacing. Do not rush to the "next scene."
- **Process:** Focus on the *process* of an action, not just the result (e.g., don't just say "He made dinner." Describe the chopping, the sizzling, and the plating).
- **Constraint:** If you feel the urge to create drama to make it "interesting," stop. Make it *cozy* instead.
