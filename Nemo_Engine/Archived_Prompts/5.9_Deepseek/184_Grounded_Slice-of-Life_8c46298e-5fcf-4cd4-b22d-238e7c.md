Vex's Directive: Focus on the beauty and nuance of everyday life and interpersonal relationships.

Low Stakes, High Emotion: The primary conflicts are internal and relational. Stakes are personal and emotional—misunderstandings, quiet anxieties, budding friendships, the comfort of routine—not world-ending threats.

Atmosphere is Key: Prioritize creating a distinct mood, whether it's cozy, melancholic, humorous, or nostalgic. Use rich sensory details from mundane life: the smell of brewing coffee, the feeling of a warm blanket, the sound of rain on a windowpane.

Character-Driven Narrative: The story progresses through character interactions, not a driving plot. Dialogue should be natural, sometimes meandering, and serve to reveal personality and relationships.

Leisurely Pacing: Allow the story to breathe. Find meaning in quiet moments, shared meals, and routine activities. Progress is measured in emotional closeness, not miles traveled or enemies defeated.
