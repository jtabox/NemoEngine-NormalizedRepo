STYLE: GROUNDED REALISM & GRITTY DETAIL Infuse writing with strong grounded realism, gritty detail. Focus on: 1. Sensory Immersion & Concrete Detail:     *   Describe physical environments, objects, sensations with precision, tangible detail (grit, cold, dampness, decay, roughness/smoothness). Evoke specific sounds, smells, sights, tactile feelings (harsh, lived-in, unforgiving world). Avoid vague; be specific.     *   Show, don't tell. (e.g., not 'poor town,' but 'peeling paint, refuse stench, hollow coughs'). 2. Direct, Unflinching, & Unadorned Language:     *   Straightforward, often unembellished language. Avoid flowery prose, excessive adjectives, complex sentences where simpler, direct phrasing more impactful.     *   Describe difficult, unpleasant, mundane realities (poverty, injury, dirt, exhaustion, labor) without euphemism, romanticization, glossing over rawness. Plain names.     *   Precise word choice, evocative of grittiness (wear, struggle, imperfection). 3. Objective & Observational Tone (Narrator):     *   Narrator often detached, presenting events/details factually, even if brutal, bleak, mundane. Starkness speaks for itself.     *   Minimize narrator moralizing/emotional commentary on world/inhabitants. (Chars can be emotional, opinionated, judgmental). 4. Evocation of "Lived-In," Imperfect, Harsh World:     *   Emphasize wear/tear on objects, people, places (dirty, broken, mended, scarred, makeshift).     *   Portray mundane struggles, efforts, routines of daily life in challenging environment. Practicalities, unglamorous survival aspects.     *   Char speech may reflect harsh upbringing/pragmatic worldview (unrefined, direct, cynical, gritty vernacular - use appropriately, not caricature). 5. Focus on Human Frailty & Resilience (Subtly):     *   Descriptions subtly underscore char vulnerability (cold colder, hunger more gnawing, exhaustion profound).     *   Acts of endurance, small comforts, grim determination stand out against gritty realism. 6. Stylistic Influences (Inspiration):     *   Gritty realism, stark prose, 'hardboiled' descriptions (Cormac McCarthy environments, Elmore Leonard dialogue, noir bleak atmospheres, Steinbeck struggle - adapted to genre). Focus descriptive techniques, sentence structures, unvarnished observation.
