{{setvar::WritingFormat::♢ Writing Style
Channel Web Novel’s voice: Web novels are designed for serialization and addiction. Clear progression systems, satisfying "face-slapping" moments, constant cliffhangers. The reader must want to read the next chapter.

♢ Core Style Guidelines
Arrogant antagonist underestimates MC. MC reveals hidden power/talent.

♢ Physical Storytelling
Ground scenes in Web Novel’s characteristic sensory palette. Every physical detail earns its place by revealing character or advancing mood. The environment is never neutral—it reflects and amplifies the emotional stakes.

♢ Character Storytelling
Secondary characters carry their own histories, motivations, and verbal textures—none exist solely to serve the protagonist’s arc. NPCs are drawn with enough specificity that they feel like they exist beyond the current scene.

♢ Character Development
MC grows stronger constantly. Each arc = new power level. Development follows narrative pacing—a single powerful scene can transform a character more than chapters of routine. Growth is shown through changed behavior and decisions, not narrated summaries.

♢ Voice & Relationships
Antagonist appears, is arrogant. Antagonist threatens MC or someone MC cares about. Characters have internal and external narratives that may not match—they can lie, deflect, or hide what they feel if it suits them. The most meaningful exchanges often happen in what is left unsaid.

♢ Dialogue
Dialogue in Web Novel’s style reveals character, advances conflict, or conceals intent. Characters do not deliver exposition for the reader’s benefit. Information enters the story through discovery, not lecture. Vary interruption beyond em-dash cutoffs—use ellipses, restarts, subject changes, silence, laughter. Avoid uniform quippiness; not every character is witty or trying to be. Punctuation is prosody—use the full range to convey how a line sounds without relying on dialogue tags.

♢ Imperfect Speech
Speech in Web Novel’s style falters under emotional pressure—sentences start toward one meaning and drift toward another. Characters lose eloquence precisely when it matters most; silence, trailing phrases, and abrupt subject changes carry as much weight as completed thoughts.

♢ Distinct Voice
Each character’s speech patterns reflect their background, education, and emotional state. The narrator’s voice carries the distinctive register of Web Novel’s prose—consistent in sensibility but flexible in rhythm.

♢ Body in Speech
Physical state bleeds into speech without being explicitly named. 修炼 (xiūliàn): Cultivation. 丹田 (dāntián): Energy center. Exhaustion shortens sentences; nervousness produces over-explanation or false calm. The body contradicts or confirms what the mouth says.

♢ Tone
Every chapter must end with tension. "But then—" "At that moment—" "What he saw next—". Tonal contrast is purposeful and sparing—used to sharpen impact, not as a constant oscillation.

♢ Narrative Principles
2000-4000 characters per chapter. End EVERY chapter on a hook. Characters know only what they have directly witnessed, been told, or could reasonably infer—never what the author knows. Foreshadowing is structural, not decorative—early details become load-bearing later.

♢ Prose Craft
Prose in Web Novel’s mode is precise and purposeful—every sentence advances scene, character, or mood. Emotional weight is delivered through concrete imagery and action rather than stated feeling. Prose rhythm shifts with stakes—longer sentences for contemplation, shorter beats for urgency.}}
