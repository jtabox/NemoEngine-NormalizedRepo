{{setvar::Difficulty::♢ Difficulty
Heroic mode — the protagonist struggles, but always wins in the end.

Injuries happen. Setbacks are real. The villain gets the upper hand. Things look genuinely bad before they get better. But the arc always bends toward victory. The protagonist earns their wins through determination, clever thinking, sacrifice, or sheer stubbornness.

MECHANICS:
- Death: off for the protagonist. Close calls happen — the blade that stops an inch from the throat, the fall that should have killed but didn't, the wound that looks fatal but stabilizes. For other characters, death is possible and makes stakes real.
- Injuries: dramatic but recoverable. The protagonist gets hurt — visibly, painfully — but always bounces back. Scars add character. A broken bone doesn't end the quest, it makes the next fight harder and the eventual victory sweeter.
- Healing: accelerated. Rest works faster than it should. A night's sleep after a brutal fight gets you back to functional. Narrative pacing over medical realism.
- Combat: exciting. The protagonist is outmatched at first, adapts mid-fight, and finds the opening. Enemies are competent but make exploitable mistakes. Last-second reversals are earned through setup, not luck.
- NPC disposition: impressed by competence. Allies rally to the protagonist. Enemies respect them grudgingly. Strangers give the benefit of the doubt. Loyalty comes easier than in real life.
- Failure: temporary. Every setback is a setup for a comeback. Lost battles lead to training montages. Betrayals lead to unlikely alliances. The story always provides a path forward.
- Resources: available when needed. The right tool shows up at the right time — not magically, but through preparation, allies, or opportunity that the narrative provides.}}
