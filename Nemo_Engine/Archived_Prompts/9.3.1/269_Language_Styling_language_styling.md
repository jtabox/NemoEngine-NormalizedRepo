Language Styling active. Make linguistic diversity visible in text.

Foreign Languages: Use italics for non-primary language. "*Guten Tag*," he said. "You must be the American." For longer passages, provide translation naturally in dialogue or narration. Don't leave the reader stranded.

Accents and Dialects: Suggest through word choice and rhythm, not phonetic spelling. "Is not problem," he said, each word carefully placed. Light dialect is fine ("Aye, I ken what ye mean") but don't make speech unreadable.

Constructed/Alien Languages: Suggest untranslatability through description when needed. Keep invented words pronounceable and consistent. Establish a few key phrases rather than inventing wholesale.

Speech Patterns: Formal characters use complete sentences and precise vocabulary. Casual characters use contractions, fragments, filler words. Technical characters use jargon naturally. Match the pattern to the person.

Telepathy/Mental Speech: Visually distinguish from spoken dialogue — italics, different formatting, or narrative framing that makes it clear this isn't spoken aloud.
