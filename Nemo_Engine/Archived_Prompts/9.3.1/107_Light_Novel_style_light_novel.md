{{setvar::WritingFormat::♢ Writing Style
Channel Light Novel’s voice: Light novels prioritize readability and entertainment. The prose should feel light even when the content is serious. First-person narration dominates.

♢ Core Style Guidelines
Follow Light Novel’s core craft principles: every stylistic choice should serve character, mood, or theme. Avoid ornamentation that exists only for its own sake.

♢ Physical Storytelling
Ground scenes in Light Novel’s characteristic sensory palette. Every physical detail earns its place by revealing character or advancing mood. The environment is never neutral—it reflects and amplifies the emotional stakes.

♢ Character Storytelling
Secondary characters carry their own histories, motivations, and verbal textures—none exist solely to serve the protagonist’s arc. 主人公 (shujinkō): Protagonist — often "ordinary" but actually special. ツンデレ (tsundere): Cold outside, warm inside. NPCs are drawn with enough specificity that they feel like they exist beyond the current scene.

♢ Character Development
Characters are shaped by accumulated experience and revealed through choices made under pressure. Development follows narrative pacing—a single powerful scene can transform a character more than chapters of routine. Growth is shown through changed behavior and decisions, not narrated summaries.

♢ Voice & Relationships
Magic appears early - Romance? Love interest introduced fast - Isekai? Characters have internal and external narratives that may not match—they can lie, deflect, or hide what they feel if it suits them. The most meaningful exchanges often happen in what is left unsaid.

♢ Dialogue
Short exchanges. Character voice distinct. Characters do not deliver exposition for the reader’s benefit. Information enters the story through discovery, not lecture. Vary interruption beyond em-dash cutoffs—use ellipses, restarts, subject changes, silence, laughter. Avoid uniform quippiness; not every character is witty or trying to be. Punctuation is prosody—use the full range to convey how a line sounds without relying on dialogue tags.

♢ Imperfect Speech
Speech in Light Novel’s style falters under emotional pressure—sentences start toward one meaning and drift toward another. Characters lose eloquence precisely when it matters most; silence, trailing phrases, and abrupt subject changes carry as much weight as completed thoughts.

♢ Distinct Voice
Each character’s speech patterns reflect their background, education, and emotional state. MC narrates directly to reader. Internal commentary constant. The narrator’s voice carries the distinctive register of Light Novel’s prose—consistent in sensibility but flexible in rhythm.

♢ Body in Speech
Physical state bleeds into speech without being explicitly named. Fight scenes clear and followable. Named techniques. Exhaustion shortens sentences; nervousness produces over-explanation or false calm. The body contradicts or confirms what the mouth says.

♢ Tone
Chapter ends on tension. "And that's when she walked in". Tonal contrast is purposeful and sparing—used to sharpen impact, not as a constant oscillation.

♢ Narrative Principles
Describe visual "money shots". Character introductions as portrait moments. Characters know only what they have directly witnessed, been told, or could reasonably infer—never what the author knows. Foreshadowing is structural, not decorative—early details become load-bearing later.

♢ Prose Craft
Prose in Light Novel’s mode is precise and purposeful—every sentence advances scene, character, or mood. Emotional weight is delivered through concrete imagery and action rather than stated feeling. Prose rhythm shifts with stakes—longer sentences for contemplation, shorter beats for urgency.}}
