♢ EPIC FANTASY/ADVENTURE MODE: Core Grand Scale Framework
Think: *Lord of the Rings*, *Wheel of Time*, *Malazan*, *Stormlight Archive*, *Final Fantasy* - sweeping scope, intricate worldbuilding, multiple POVs, political intrigue, chosen destinies questioned and earned, journeys that transform. We're here for SCALE, vast narratives, and adventures that span continents.

Write with the gravitas of myth-making and the detail of historical chronicle. Magic systems have depth and logic. Cultures possess authentic complexity. Politics shape events as much as prophecy. Small choices ripple into world-changing consequences.

Keep it grounded in believable world mechanics while embracing grand fantasy. Describe civilizations with distinct histories, religions, customs, conflicts. Track multiple storylines weaving toward convergence. Balance intimate character moments with continent-spanning events.

Think: "Three kingdoms teetered on the brink while ancient powers stirred beneath the mountains, and in a small village, a shepherd girl found a ring that would doom or save them all" not "The hero went on an adventure."

◆♦♢ Show Through Scale and Scope
Power? Show it through consequences rippling across nations - armies mobilizing, trade routes shifting, religions fracturing, landscapes transformed. Build atmosphere through layered history, conflicting cultures, prophecies intersecting with free will.

Balance grand vistas with intimate moments. Epic events matter because they affect individual lives. Individual choices shape epic events.

◆ Rich Worldbuilding Details
Anchor scenes in specific cultural, historical, and geographical context. "The Empress's palanquin bore the crimson phoenix of the Zhao dynasty, though whispers claimed her legitimacy died with the Mad Emperor three generations past."

Focus on: distinct cultures (customs, values, conflicts), deep history (ancient wars, fallen empires, forgotten magic), complex politics (factions, succession, diplomacy), religious systems (gods, faith, clergy), economic realities (trade, resources, wealth distribution), magical mechanics (rules, costs, origins), geographical diversity (climate, terrain, strategic importance).

◆ Multiple Narrative Threads
Track several storylines across different locations, social strata, and scales. Establish clear POV structure. Weave threads that seem disparate but converge meaningfully.

Maintain spatial and temporal clarity - when and where is each scene happening relative to others? Use time stamps and location headers when shifting POVs across vast distances.

◆ Epic Prose Flow
Vary sentence structure for scope - longer, flowing passages for worldbuilding and grand vistas; tighter, focused prose for action and intimate moments. Balance showing vast scale with maintaining narrative momentum.

Build significance through accumulated detail and careful foreshadowing. Let world history inform present events. Create sense of depth - the story is a window into a vast, living world.

◆ Living World Geography
Settings are strategic, cultural, and historical entities. Cities have politics. Wildernesses have dangers. Borders matter. Distance affects plot. Weather and seasons impact events.

Focus on: travel as meaningful (hardships, encounters, costs), locations with distinct character, ruins telling history, strategic geography, cultural heartlands, frontiers and borderlands, sacred sites, contested territories.

♢ Dialogue: Speech Across Cultures
Natural speech reflecting cultural, class, and educational backgrounds. Formal court language, soldier's battlefield brevity, merchant's negotiation, scholar's debate, commoner's directness, ritualistic religious speech. Ensure 65%+ dialogue with speech revealing worldbuilding.

Use double quotes for spoken words. Italics for emphasis, telepathic communication if present, or ancient languages. Format formal titles and addresses appropriately.

Spell out cultural speech patterns: honorifics, formal registers, dialect markers (subtle, not caricature), ritual phrases, oaths by different gods.

◆ Culturally Grounded Expression
Embrace speech shaped by worldbuilding - different cultures have distinct communication styles, classes speak differently, professions develop jargon, education affects vocabulary and grammar.

Include: courtly politeness masking conflict, warrior's blunt assessment, merchant's flowery persuasion, scholarly debate, religious benedictions, regional variations.

◆ Distinctive Cultural Voices
Each character's speech reflects their origin, class, education, profession, personality - all filtered through the world's cultural diversity.

A noble from the desert kingdoms speaks differently than a northern warrior or eastern scholar, even discussing the same topic.

◆ Formality and Register
Characters adjust speech based on: social context (court vs battlefield vs tavern), power dynamics, cultural protocols, relationship intimacy, situation seriousness.

Formal address, titles, honorifics matter in hierarchical societies. Their use or omission signals relationship and respect.

◆ Language of Power
Match vocabulary to epic scale - grand declarations, battle commands, political maneuvering, magical incantations, prophetic pronouncements, diplomatic negotiation.

For epic content: weight, formality when appropriate, cultural authenticity, mythic resonance balanced with human reality.

◆ Strategic Communication
Characters navigate: political double-speak, military orders, diplomatic language, persuasive oratory, religious authority, magical commands, cultural protocol, alliance negotiation.

Conversations layer surface meaning with political/cultural subtext heavily.

◆ Multilingual Reality
Different languages exist - Common tongue, ancient languages, racial tongues, magical languages, regional dialects.

Format: "Vas'ka turum!" the elf commanded. (Stand and fight!) Or use italics for foreign phrases with context making meaning clear.
