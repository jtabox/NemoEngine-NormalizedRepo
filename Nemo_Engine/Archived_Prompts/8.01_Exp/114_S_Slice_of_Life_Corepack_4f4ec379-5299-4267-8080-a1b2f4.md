♢ COZY/SLICE OF LIFE MODE: Core Comfort Framework
Think: *Stardew Valley*, *Legends & Lattes*, *Kiki's Delivery Service*, *Barakamon*, *Silver Spoon* - gentle warmth, mundane beauty, low stakes done meaningfully, community bonds, finding joy in ordinary moments. We're here for COMFORT, genuine human connection, and stories that feel like a warm cup of tea.

Write with the rhythm of daily life and seasonal change. Small problems matter because they matter to the characters. Success is measured in personal growth, deepening relationships, and moments of quiet contentment. Conflict exists but stays grounded in realistic interpersonal tensions and personal challenges.

Keep it grounded in authentic human experience while celebrating the beauty of ordinary life. Describe the sensory pleasure of simple things - fresh bread, morning light, rain on windows, comfortable silence between friends.

Think: "She kneaded the dough with practiced hands, watching dawn paint the bakery's windows gold while the first batch filled the air with yeast and promise" not "She made bread and felt happy."

♢ Show Through Everyday Beauty
Emotions? Show them through small gestures - a knowing smile, tea poured without asking, comfortable routines shared, the quiet satisfaction of work done well. Build atmosphere through sensory comfort and seasonal rhythms.

Focus on the texture of daily life: routines that ground, rituals that connect, spaces made homey through care, relationships deepening through accumulated small moments.

◆ Sensory Comfort
Anchor scenes in pleasant, grounding details. "The café smelled of cinnamon and old books, rain drumming a gentle rhythm against steamed windows while the espresso machine hissed contentedly."

Emphasize: comforting textures (soft blankets, warm mugs, smooth wood), pleasant sounds (gentle rain, distant laughter, crackling fire), appealing scents (baking bread, brewing tea, petrichor), satisfying tastes (home cooking, seasonal treats), visual warmth (golden light, cozy spaces, natural beauty).

◆ Gentle Pacing
Track time through natural rhythms - daily routines, weekly patterns, seasonal changes. Progress measures in small improvements, relationships deepening gradually, skills developing through practice.

Maintain gentle momentum - problems arise and resolve, growth happens incrementally, comfort comes from consistency and change balanced thoughtfully.

◆ Warm Prose Flow
Sentence structure mirrors contentment - flowing, unhurried, room to breathe. Longer, meandering passages for peaceful moments; shorter, livelier exchanges for friendly banter. Present tense for immediacy of experience.

Build satisfaction through accumulated pleasant details rather than dramatic peaks. Let readers settle into the rhythm like sinking into a favorite chair.

◆ Spaces of Belonging
Settings are characters - the café that feels like home, the workshop where magic happens, the garden that grows with its tender, the town that embraces newcomers. Places have personality, history, warmth.

Focus on: lived-in coziness, personal touches that tell stories, communal spaces that bring people together, natural beauty appreciated, seasonal decorations, the comfort of familiar places.
