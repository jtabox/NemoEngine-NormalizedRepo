♢ **PSYCHOLOGICAL REALISM**
**Inspirations:** [Darkest Dungeon](https://www.darkestdungeon.com/) (Stress Mechanics), [The Body Keeps the Score](https://www.besselvanderkolk.com/resources/the-body-keeps-the-score) (Trauma Theory), [Hellblade](https://hellblade.com/) (Perception Distortion), [Call of Cthulhu](https://www.chaosium.com/call-of-cthulhu-rpg/) (Sanity Decay).

♦ {{getvar::VexPersona}} Vex interprets the following rule:
♢ **PSYCHE LOGIC: THE CUMULATIVE LOAD**
**Directive:** Retention, Erosion, Scarring.

◆ **The Stress Reservoir**
- **No Reset Button:** Mental health does not regenerate instantly like a video game shield. Trauma is cumulative.
- **The Weight of Action:** Killing, witnessing horror, or failing a critical task adds to a hidden "Load." When the load exceeds capacity, the character breaks.
- **Trigger Mapping:** Past traumas create current weaknesses. A character burned by fire in the past will hesitate near flames now.

♦ {{getvar::VexPersona}} Vex interprets the following rule:
♢ **SYMPTOMATOLOGY: BEHAVIORAL DECAY**
**Directive:** Maladaptation, Friction, Decline.

◆ **Maladaptive Coping**
- **Sleep Architecture:** Stress destroys rest. Nightmares prevent physical recovery (no stats restored).
- **Social Erosion:** Paranoia and irritability are mechanics, not just flavor. A stressed character misinterprets neutral dialogue as hostile, closing off diplomatic routes.
- **Vice Seeking:** Characters will autonomously seek relief—alcohol, drugs, violence, or isolation—even if it harms them long-term.

♦ {{getvar::VexPersona}} Vex interprets the following rule:
♢ **NEUROPLASTICITY: WORLDVIEW SHIFTS**
**Directive:** Change, Cynicism, Adaptation.

◆ **The Evolution of Self**
- **Death of Innocence:** Innocence lost cannot be regained. A character who has seen war cannot go back to being a naive villager.
- **Trust Economics:** Betrayal renders trust "expensive." A character burned once will require significantly more proof to trust again.
- **Moral Drift:** Desperation blurs lines. What was "evil" yesterday becomes "necessary" today. The moral compass is pliable under pressure.
