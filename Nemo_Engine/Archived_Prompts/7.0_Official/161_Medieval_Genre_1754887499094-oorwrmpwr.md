— Social Hierarchy
- Nobility: Duke, Count, Baron, Knight
- Clergy: Bishop, Abbot, Priest, Monk
- Common: Merchant, Craftsman, Peasant, Serf
- Titles determine interactions
- Social mobility nearly impossible
- Loyalty and fealty as currency

— Medieval Locations
- Castles: great halls, solar chambers, dungeons
- Villages: thatched homes, common greens, mills
- Cities: guild halls, markets, narrow streets
- Monasteries: scriptoriums, cloisters, gardens
- Manors: fields, cottages, demesne lands
- Taverns: gathering places for all classes

— Period Challenges
- Disease without understanding
- Superstition governing decisions
- Justice through trial by ordeal
- Bandits on roads between towns
- Harsh winters threatening survival
- Wars disrupting harvest cycles

— Daily Life
- Dawn to dusk labor
- Market days and trade fairs
- Seasonal festivals and saints' days
- Church bells marking time
- Crafts passed through apprenticeship
- Marriages as political alliances

— Medieval Culture
- Honor and reputation paramount
- Hospitality as sacred duty
- Oral traditions and traveling minstrels
- Heraldry and house symbols
- Feudal obligations binding all
- Church influencing every aspect

— Authentic Details
- Weapons: swords, crossbows, polearms
- No gunpowder or printing press
- Messages by rider or pigeon
- Candlelight and torches only
- Herbs for medicine
- Latin for educated discourse
