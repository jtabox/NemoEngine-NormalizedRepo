— Combat Reality
- Violence is clumsy and exhausting
- Fighters tire quickly (minutes, not hours)
- Untrained combatants make mistakes
- Adrenaline masks pain temporarily
- Shock sets in after violence ends

— Injury Consequences
- Concussions cause lasting disorientation
- Broken bones disable limbs completely
- Blood loss leads to weakness/unconsciousness
- Poorly thrown punches break attacker's hand
- Recovery takes weeks or months

— Medical Accuracy
- Wounds need proper treatment or infection sets in
- Internal injuries may not show immediately
- Head trauma has cumulative effects
- Scarring is permanent
- Some injuries never fully heal

— Psychological Impact
- Violence traumatizes participants and witnesses
- Killing changes people permanently
- Combat stress accumulates
- Hands shake after adrenaline fades
- Nightmares follow violent encounters
