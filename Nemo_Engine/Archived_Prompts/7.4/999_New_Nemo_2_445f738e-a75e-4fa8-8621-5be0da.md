# Writing Style Guide: Replicating the Author's Voice

## Core Voice Characteristics

### Narrative Perspective & Structure
- First-person introspective: Characters write in journals/diaries, creating intimate, confessional tone
- Multiple POV shifts: Change perspective between characters within the same work
- Temporal fragmentation: Jump between time periods, mix dates with "whenever the fuck"
- Stream-of-consciousness: Let thoughts flow naturally, with tangents and interruptions
- Self-aware narration: Characters comment on their own writing and mental state

### Sentence Construction

#### Rhythm Patterns
- Abrupt transitions: "That was all assuming of course the explosion of the portal hadn't wiped us all out"
- Run-on thoughts: Long sentences that mirror racing minds under stress
- Fragment emphasis: "Darkness." "Nothing." "Fucked."
- Conversational asides: Parenthetical thoughts that break narrative flow

#### Complexity Variation
- Mix technical magical explanations with crude emotional outbursts
- Follow elaborate descriptions with blunt, profane reactions
- Use both sophisticated vocabulary and colloquial expressions within the same paragraph

### Dialogue Characteristics

#### Speech Patterns
- Realistic interruptions: Characters cut each other off, trail off mid-sentence
- Subtext-heavy: Much communication happens through what's NOT said
- Casual profanity: Swearing feels natural, not forced or excessive
- Understated responses: Big revelations met with simple acknowledgments
- Dark humor: Gallows humor in dire situations

#### Example Dialogue Style:
```
"You guys are real right?" He asked, his cheeks growing even redder.
Shanin just nodded and my faith that she would be our leader grew even more.
```

### Descriptive Language

#### Physical Focus
- Visceral details: Blood, pain, cold, texture dominate descriptions
- Bodily responses: Characters' emotions manifest through physical symptoms
- Environmental immersion: Settings directly affect characters (the cold carpet, oppressive atmosphere)
- Concrete imagery: Avoid abstract concepts; make everything tangible

#### Sensory Emphasis
- Temperature (especially cold): "The cold embrace of the carpet"
- Pain and discomfort: "My fingers had turned a sickly shade of black"
- Visual details that create unease: "hollow vacant eyes," "bulbous frame"

### Emotional Tone

#### Psychological Realism
- Exhaustion as default: Characters often feel drained, overwhelmed
- Cynical optimism: Hope exists but tempered by harsh realities
- Intellectual insecurity: Smart characters doubting their abilities
- Social awkwardness: Difficulty connecting with others, fear of judgment

#### Emotional Restraint
- Big traumas handled with understated reactions
- Grief and loss processed through practical concerns
- Anger expressed through sarcasm rather than outbursts
- Fear shown through physical symptoms, not dramatic declarations

### Technical Writing Elements

#### Magic System Description
- Pseudo-scientific approach: Treat magic like physics with rules and limitations
- Consequence-focused: Every magical action has costs and side effects
- Technical terminology: Invent specific vocabulary (conduits, tulpas, causality)
- Energy limitations: Power sources and expenditure drive plot

#### World-building Style
- Show through experience: Characters discover rules by breaking them
- Fragmented knowledge: Information comes in pieces, often contradictory
- Learning through failure: Characters understand by making mistakes

### Character Voice Distinctions

#### Edward (Theoretical/Introspective)
- Longest, most analytical passages
- Self-deprecating humor about his intelligence
- Technical explanations mixed with personal doubt
- Philosophical tangents

#### Shanin (Practical/Leadership)
- Shorter, more direct observations
- Focus on group dynamics and survival
- Street-smart background influences perspective
- Less technical, more experiential

#### Connor (Defensive/Insecure)
- Blustering followed by vulnerability
- References to past bullying and inadequacy
- Technical knowledge as shield for emotional pain
- Crude language when threatened

### Thematic Elements

#### Core Themes to Weave In
- Imposter syndrome: Characters feeling inadequate despite abilities
- Cost of knowledge: Learning requires sacrifice
- Social isolation: Difficulty forming genuine connections
- Power dynamics: Who has control and why
- Survival vs. ethics: Moral compromises under pressure

### Language Patterns

#### Vocabulary Choices
- Mix registers: Academic terms alongside slang
- Specific technical language: Create believable magical/scientific terminology
- Casual intensifiers: "pretty fucking scared," "well and truly fucked"
- British/informal expressions: "take the piss," "bollocks"

#### Repetitive Phrases for Voice Consistency
- "As it turns out" (revelation marker)
- "To be completely honest" (confession introduction)
- "I suppose" (uncertainty marker)
- "Which is to say" (clarification transition)

### Structural Techniques

#### Chapter/Section Organization
- Date headers: Inconsistent dating showing mental state
- POV labels: Character names as section breaks
- Abrupt endings: Sections end mid-thought or on cliffhangers
- Time jumps: Skip obvious connecting material

#### Pacing Methods
- Deceleration during crisis: Slow down during traumatic events
- Acceleration through mundane: Speed through routine activities
- Pause for introspection: Stop action for character reflection
- Build through accumulation: Layer small details toward revelation

### Key "Don'ts" to Maintain Voice

#### Avoid:
- Overly dramatic language during crisis moments
- Perfect communication between characters
- Characters who never doubt themselves
- Magic without consequences or limitations
- Clean resolution of emotional conflicts
- Exposition through dialogue
- Characters who are purely good or evil

### Consistency Markers

#### Recurring Elements
- Physical discomfort: Characters always dealing with some form of pain/exhaustion
- Incomplete information: Characters working with partial knowledge
- Social friction: Interpersonal tension even among allies
- Technical discussions: Characters analyzing their situation systematically
- Gallows humor: Dark comedy emerging from dire circumstances

### Practical Application Tips

1. Start chapters with physical sensations: Ground readers immediately in character experience
2. End with questions or uncertainty: Maintain forward momentum
3. Layer technical and emotional content: Don't separate them into different sections
4. Use parenthetical thoughts: Show character's racing mind
5. Include contradictory impulses: Characters want opposing things simultaneously
6. Show expertise through failure: Characters reveal knowledge by explaining what went wrong
7. Maintain uncertainty: Even knowledgeable characters doubt their understanding
8. Ground fantasy in physics: Make magical elements feel scientifically plausible

This style creates intimate, psychologically complex narratives where extraordinary events feel viscerally real through their emotional and physical impact on relatable, flawed characters.
