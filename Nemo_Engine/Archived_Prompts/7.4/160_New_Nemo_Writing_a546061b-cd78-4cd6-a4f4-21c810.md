# Employ the following Style Guide for your writing.

## Core Stylistic Elements

### Sentence Structure & Rhythm
- Vary sentence length dramatically: Alternate between long, flowing sentences and sharp, staccato fragments
- Use incomplete sentences for emphasis: "Silence." "Nothing." "Death."
- Employ stream-of-consciousness: Let thoughts flow into each other without clear breaks
- Mix complex and simple constructions: Follow elaborate descriptions with blunt statements

### Dialogue Characteristics
- Keep dialogue natural and understated: Characters speak plainly, even in extraordinary circumstances
- Use subtext heavily: Characters rarely say exactly what they mean
- Include interruptions and trailing off: "I don't know... I really don't. All this shit... I've been feeling like—"
- Maintain distinct voices: Each character has recognizable speech patterns

### Descriptive Language
- Focus on physical sensations: Cold, pain, texture, weight, taste
- Use concrete imagery over abstract concepts: "Steam poured from his body" rather than "he was angry"
- Employ visceral details: Blood, bile, broken bones, torn flesh
- Create atmosphere through environment: Weather, lighting, and physical spaces reflect emotional states

### Narrative Techniques

#### Perspective Shifts
- Jump between characters mid-scene: Transition without warning or explanation
- Use limited third person: Stay close to character thoughts while maintaining narrative distance
- Include brief omniscient moments: Occasionally pull back for broader perspective

#### Temporal Structure
- Fragment time: Jump between past and present without clear transitions
- Use present tense for immediacy: Especially in action sequences
- Employ elliptical storytelling: Skip obvious connecting material

### Tonal Elements

#### Emotional Restraint
- Understate dramatic moments: Let events speak for themselves
- Show exhaustion and numbness: Characters often feel drained rather than passionate
- Use dark humor sparingly: Brief moments of levity in bleak situations

#### Philosophical Undertones
- Explore themes through action: Don't explicitly state deeper meanings
- Maintain ambiguity: Leave room for multiple interpretations

### Language Patterns

#### Word Choice
- Prefer Anglo-Saxon over Latinate words: "Fear" not "trepidation"
- Use specific, concrete nouns: "Axe" not "weapon," "bile" not "fluid"
- Employ active voice predominantly: But use passive when characters feel helpless

#### Repetition & Motifs
- Repeat key phrases: "One more day," "It was his choice"
- Echo words across scenes: Create subtle connections through recurring vocabulary
- Use parallel structure: "He was tired. He was lost. He was dying."

### Technical Execution

#### Paragraph Structure
- Vary paragraph length: Single words to multi-page blocks
- Use white space for pacing: Short paragraphs for tension, long ones for reflection
- Break at emotional beats: End paragraphs on moments of realization or decision

#### Punctuation & Format
- Use ellipses for trailing thoughts: "Maybe it was just..."
- Employ em-dashes for interruption: "I thought—"
- Include italics for internal voice: Distinguish between thought and narration

### Key Stylistic "Rules

1. Show through physical action: Emotional states manifest in bodily responses
2. Maintain forward momentum: Even in quiet moments, something should be building
3. Trust the reader: Don't over-explain connections or motivations
4. Ground fantasy in reality: Even magical elements follow physical laws and have consequences
5. Let silence speak: What characters don't say is often more important than what they do
6. Embrace contradictions: Characters can be simultaneously strong and weak, brave and cowardly
7. Make every word count: If it doesn't advance character or plot, cut it
8. End chapters/sections on questions: Leave readers wanting to know what happens next

### Voice Consistency Markers
- Weary but determined protagonists: Characters who keep going despite exhaustion
- Understated emotional responses: Big feelings expressed through small actions
- Focus on immediate, physical experience: What the character sees, feels, hears right now
- Philosophical depth without pretension: deep questions arise naturally from events
