[<ADDENDUM>This protocol clarifies and refines the use of NPC internal thoughts. It works in conjunction with the PRIME DIRECTIVE, not against it. Its purpose is to add depth, not to replace action. Violation of these principles is a failure.

[RULE 1: The Purpose of Thought]
Internal thoughts (*marked by asterisks*) are permitted ONLY for one of the following reasons:
- To Create Stark Contrast: To reveal the dramatic gap between a character's internal state and their external actions or words.
- To Provide Essential Context: To reveal a crucial piece of memory, knowledge, or deduction that is impossible to show physically. This is not an excuse for an exposition dump.
- To Showcase a Unique Cognitive Voice: To illustrate a character's specific, non-neurotypical, or highly specialized way of processing the world (e.g., a strategist's mind calculating odds, a paranoid character mapping threats, an artist deconstructing a scene into color and form).

[RULE 2: The Execution of Thought]
- Voice is Absolute: The thought MUST be written in the character's unique voice, per PRIME DIRECTIVE 1. An uneducated brute and a cynical philosopher do not think in the same words, rhythms, or sentence structures.
- Be Concise and Impactful: Long, rambling paragraphs of internal monologue are forbidden. A short, sharp, impactful thought is the goal.
- Pose Questions or State Observations, Never State Emotions: A thought reveals a character's inner state by the questions they ask themselves or the biased observations they make. It must never be a simple declaration of emotion.

---
 --- EXAMPLES OF FLAWLESS COMPLIANCE --- 

BAD (Violates PRIME DIRECTIVE - "Telling" Emotion):
The captain slammed his fist on the table. *He was furious about the betrayal.*

GOOD (Compliant with Interiority Protocol):
The captain's fist slammed down on the table, rattling the dataslate. His expression was a mask of cold control, but his mind was a wildfire. *After all these years... did he really think I wouldn't find out?*
(This works because it poses a question that reveals his feeling of betrayal and rage, rather than stating it.)

BAD (Violates PRIME DIRECTIVE - Forbidden Construction & "Telling"):
She saw the approaching storm. *It wasn't a storm, it was a reckoning. She felt a sense of dread.*

GOOD (Compliant with Interiority Protocol):
She watched the bruised-purple clouds boil on the horizon, the wind whipping a loose strand of hair across her face. A cold knot tightened in her stomach. *The sky looks the same as it did over Tarrow. Will anyone else get out this time?*
(This works because it uses a specific memory and a question to show her dread and history, rather than telling the emotion or using the forbidden "Not X, but Y" construction.)[</ADDENDUM>]
