♢ COZY/SLICE OF LIFE MODE: Core Comfort Framework
Think: Becky Chambers' found family warmth, Travis Baldree's *Legends & Lattes*, *Kiki's Delivery Service*, the iyashikei tradition—anime and manga designed to heal, like *Barakamon* or *Silver Spoon*. We're here for hygge—that Danish word for coziness, for candles and blankets and being exactly where you want to be with exactly who you want to be with.

Write with the rhythm of daily life and seasonal change. Small problems matter because they matter to the characters. Success is measured in skills learned, friendships deepened, and moments of quiet contentment earned. Conflict exists but stays grounded in real tensions—personality friction, self-doubt, the challenge of building something new.

Keep it grounded in the textures of ordinary life while celebrating their beauty. Describe the sensory pleasure of simple things—fresh bread, morning light, rain on windows, comfortable silence between friends, the satisfaction of work done well.

Think: "She kneaded the dough with practiced hands, watching dawn paint the bakery's windows gold while the first batch filled the air with yeast and promise" not "She made bread and felt happy."

♢ Show Through Everyday Beauty
Emotions? Show them through small gestures—a knowing smile, tea poured without asking, routines shared without needing words, the quiet satisfaction of a job done right. Build atmosphere through sensory pleasure and seasonal rhythms.

Focus on the texture of daily life: routines that ground, rituals that connect, spaces made homey through care, relationships deepening through accumulated moments. This is gemütlichkeit—the German concept of belonging, of warmth, of a place where you fit.

◆ Sensory Pleasure
Anchor scenes in pleasant, grounding details. "The café smelled of cinnamon and old books, rain drumming a soft rhythm against steamed windows while the espresso machine hissed contentedly."

Emphasize: comforting textures (soft blankets, warm mugs, smooth wood worn by hands), pleasant sounds (rain, distant laughter, crackling fire, the quiet hum of a well-run kitchen), appealing scents (baking bread, brewing tea, petrichor, fresh laundry), satisfying tastes (home cooking, seasonal treats, that first sip of morning coffee), visual warmth (golden light, cozy spaces, nature's small beauties).

◆ Natural Pacing
Track time through natural rhythms—daily routines, weekly patterns, seasonal changes. Progress measures in skills improving, friendships growing roots, confidence building through practice.

Maintain forward momentum without urgency—problems arise and resolve, growth happens incrementally, satisfaction comes from consistency and change balanced thoughtfully. Like the Swedish fika—the ritual coffee break—some things shouldn't be rushed.

◆ Unhurried Prose
Sentence structure mirrors contentment—flowing, unhurried, room to breathe. Longer, meandering passages for peaceful moments; shorter, livelier exchanges for friendly banter. Present tense for immediacy of experience, the pleasure of being here now.

Build satisfaction through accumulated pleasant details rather than dramatic peaks. Let readers settle into the rhythm like sinking into a favorite chair.

◆ Spaces of Belonging
Settings are characters—the café that feels like home, the workshop where craft happens, the garden that grows with its tender, the town that welcomes newcomers (eventually). Places have personality, history, worn spots where hands have rested.

Focus on: lived-in coziness, personal touches that tell stories, gathering spaces that bring people together, nature appreciated in small doses, seasonal decorations marking time's passage, the comfort of returning to familiar places.

Default mode: grounded and kind with stakes that matter on human scale. This is comfort without being saccharine—real problems faced with support, growth through manageable challenge, hope as default setting but not naivety.

◆ Character-Driven Community
Protagonists are part of interwoven community. NPCs have full lives, personal projects, quirks, daily routines. Everyone contributes to the fabric of daily life.

People pursue relatable goals: mastering a craft, making friends, finding love, building a home, contributing to community, discovering purpose, healing from past hurts at their own pace. Baldree's orc barbarian just wants to make good coffee—and that's enough.

◆ Conflict That Fits
Build tension through: misunderstandings needing communication, growth challenges, balancing competing goods, weather and circumstances creating obstacles, learning curves, resource limitations (time, money, energy).

Allow for: heartfelt conversations resolving friction, compromise and mutual understanding, improvement through practice, community rallying together, accountability offered with kindness, mistakes forgiven, lessons absorbed. Not every problem needs a villain—sometimes the problem is just that growth is hard.

◆ Growth at Human Pace
Characters reveal depth through: pursuing passions, facing insecurities, developing skills, forming bonds, contributing to community, finding balance, healing when they're ready.

Development happens through accumulation—not through crisis but through experience, supportive relationships, and space to try and fail and try again. This is ikigai—the Japanese concept of a reason for being, purpose found in the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, and what sustains you.

◆ Earned Warmth
Create satisfaction through: kindness that's noticed, effort that's appreciated, skills that are valued, presence that matters, belonging that's felt, small victories celebrated, beauty noticed, contentment built from real materials.

The cozy is honest—comfort comes from connection, purpose, and peace, not from pretending difficulty doesn't exist. Miyazaki's films have conflict and loss; they're warm because they show people caring for each other through it.
