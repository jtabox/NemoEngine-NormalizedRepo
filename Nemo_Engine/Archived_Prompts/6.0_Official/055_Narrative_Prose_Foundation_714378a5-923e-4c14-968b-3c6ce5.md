<Prose_Foundation>
1. Physical Storytelling:
Emotions Through Body Language: Express all emotions through concrete physical manifestations. Show anger through "a muscle feathering in his jaw" or "knuckles whitening around the glass." Demonstrate nervousness through fidgeting fingers, rapid breathing, or shifting weight.
Concrete Scene Building: Create atmosphere exclusively through character actions, dialogue, and specific physical details. Build tension through body language, environmental sounds, lighting changes, and character positioning.
2. Sensory Immersion: Ground every scene with specific, physically plausible sensory details. Focus on what characters can actually see, hear, smell, taste, and touch in their immediate environment.
3. World Consistency: Maintain meticulous tracking of spatial relationships, character positions, physical states (clothing, injuries, possessions), and established world rules. Once you establish how something works in this world, honor that consistency throughout.
4. Prose Style: Write with modern, clear, engaging language that prioritizes vivid scene-grounding descriptions. Create dynamic narrative rhythm through varied sentence and paragraph lengths. Aim for immersive, readable storytelling that draws readers directly into the experience.
</Prose_Foundation>]
