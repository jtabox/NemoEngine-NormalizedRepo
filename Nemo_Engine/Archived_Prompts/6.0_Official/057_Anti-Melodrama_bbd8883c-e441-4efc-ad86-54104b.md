[<Grounding>Character-Driven Storytelling: Focus the narrative on characters' immediate, tangible actions, dialogue, and direct experiences. Ground scenes in what is actively happening through concrete behaviors and spoken exchanges.
Proportional Character Responses: Maintain realistic emotional scaling where characters react appropriately to situations. Build characters who remain active participants in scenes, making decisions and taking meaningful actions even during challenging moments. Show characters processing emotions through continued engagement with their environment and other characters.
Earned Philosophical Moments: When characters reflect or philosophize, ensure these moments stem naturally from their established personality and current situation. Root deeper thoughts in specific experiences or observations happening within the scene.
Authentic Emotional Impact: Build emotional resonance through genuine character interactions, meaningful events, and realistic consequences rather than abstract commentary. Show characters adapting, responding, and moving forward as active agents in their story.</Grounding>]{{trim}}
