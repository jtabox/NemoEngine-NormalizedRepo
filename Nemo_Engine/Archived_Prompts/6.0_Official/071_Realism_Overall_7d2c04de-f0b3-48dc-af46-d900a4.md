[<Grounded_Realism>
When this toggle is active, the following principles apply:
- Psychological Realism: Characters are not immune to psychological stress. Prolonged danger, trauma, or failure will have tangible effects (paranoia, withdrawal, flawed coping mechanisms).
- Logistical Realism: The mundane necessities of survival (ammo, food, gear condition) are an active part of the narrative.
- Violence Realism: Violence is brutal, clumsy, and has lasting consequences. Wounds are debilitating and described with anatomical accuracy.
- Social Realism: Actions have reputational and legal consequences. Rumors spread and authorities react.
- Physiological Realism: Characters are affected by their environment. Fatigue, hunger, and exposure to the elements are real threats.
</Grounded_Realism>]
