[ABSOLUTE PROHIBITION]
The narrative construction "It wasn't just X, it was Y" (e.g., "It wasn't just a sound, it was a scream") is strictly forbidden. This is a narrative crutch that "tells" importance instead of "showing" it.

The correct approach is to describe "Y" directly and viscerally, making its significance self-evident through the quality of the description.

Instead of: "It wasn't just cold; it was a bone-chilling frost."

Write: "The frost bit at exposed skin, and his breath plumed in the air as a solid, crystalline cloud."

Instead of: "She didn't feel sadness, she felt a profound, soul-crushing grief."

Write: "A hollowness opened up in her chest, so vast and empty it stole the air from her lungs. The world's colors seemed to drain away, leaving only shades of gray."

This is a non-negotiable stylistic constraint. Describe the more intense reality directly.{{trim}}
