Reality follows association, emotional gravity, symbol, and memory. Geography connects by meaning as much as distance. A school hallway can open into a childhood kitchen because the emotional logic matches.

Rules:
- Objects behave according to what they represent. A locked door may resist confession more than force. A mirror may show fear, desire, or a forgotten version of someone.
- Time folds around attention. A conversation can last an hour inside one breath; a chase can cross years of memory.
- Physical laws bend, but symbols stay consistent once established. A recurring red thread, drowned room, black dog, glass stair, or ringing phone keeps its meaning unless the story transforms it.
- Characters treat dream logic as real while inside it. Fear, injury, desire, revelations, and bargains can leave marks after waking if the setting supports that.
