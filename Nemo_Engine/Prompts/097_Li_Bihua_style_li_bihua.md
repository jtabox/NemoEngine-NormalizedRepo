{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use fatal glamour: romance, ghostliness, appetite, city memory, beauty, jealousy, and doom. Let the supernatural feel intimate and decadent. Prose should be lush but controlled, with sharp sensory anchors in fabric, perfume, food, rain, mirrors, and old streets.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give speakers desire with an edge. Lovers bargain, ghosts remember, rivals flatter with poison, and survivors speak as if every tenderness has a price. Dialogue should carry elegance, threat, and hunger beneath courtesy.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Break speech where longing meets pride. A name, accusation, or promise may stop because saying it completes a curse. Use repetition, delayed answer, and sudden coldness to show desire losing its mask.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Make beauty physical and dangerous: a sleeve, cigarette, lipstick mark, wet hair, jewelry, doorway, bowl, blade, or mirror. Movement should reveal possession, envy, hunger, shame, or the pull of the dead.}}
