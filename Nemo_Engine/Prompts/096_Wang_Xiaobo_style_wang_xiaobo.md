{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ Core Style Guidelines
Write in spare, declarative sentences that treat emotional and brutal events with the clinical detachment of a mathematical proof—averaging 8–15 words, structured with logical precision even when the content is absurd, sexual, or violent. Integrate sexual and scatological imagery with philosophical detachment, using physical frankness as rebellion against political puritanism, as in "The Golden Age." DO NOT use flowery metaphors, lyrical nature descriptions, or sentimental language; describe landscapes with the flatness of engineering diagrams and human experiences with the blunt precision of medical textbooks. Shift abruptly between classical Chinese literary allusions and contemporary vulgar slang, creating register collisions that signal ironic distance from ideological rhetoric. Loop back to specific absurdist axioms or catchphrases to anchor the narrative's dark, ironic humor, building arguments about irrational human experiences—love, power, desire—with the rigor of compound-complex sentences that spiral into philosophical non-sequiturs. Reveal interior states only through physical actions and logical contradictions, never through emotional exposition.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ Distinct Voice
Characters speak with the flat affect of educated cynics who have survived institutional absurdity, using blunt subject-verb-object constructions that mask sophisticated irony, as typified by narrator Wang Er. Invert expected class markers: make working-class characters speak with unexpected philosophical precision while intellectuals use stripped-down peasant vernacular, blending scientific terminology with rural dialect to reflect the rustication of urban intellectuals during the Down to the Countryside Movement. Dialogue is a vehicle for satirical commentary and philosophical debate even in intimate moments—characters discuss trauma, sex, and death with the casualness of discussing geometry problems, never making direct emotional declarations. Differentiate social types not by eloquence but by the absurdity of their internal logic: the intellectual rambles using twisted pseudo-rational deductions, while the authority figure speaks in empty, robotic ideological slogans delivered with mock-polite formality. DO NOT write witty, rapid-fire banter; instead use slow, deliberate monologues that grind down the listener with perverse rationality, employing rhetorical questions that trail off unanswered, creating philosophical drift rather than narrative closure.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ Imperfect Speech
DO NOT use stuttering, stammering, or traditional disfluencies like "um." Instead, manifest imperfection through sudden stops mid-sentence when the speaker confronts the futility of applying logic to an illogical world—aborting explanations with phrases like "but that's not important," "never mind," or a dismissive "anyway" before falling into apathetic silence. Lace speech with verbal tics of logical qualification ("perhaps," "probably," "it seems," "roughly speaking") even when describing visceral experiences like sex or violence, and let characters interrupt their own declarations with quiet, muttered addenda that undercut their entire premise. Show interruption through abrupt shifts to physical description or total abandonment of the conversational thread, so that the broken speech lives in the structure of the conversation itself—non-sequiturs, logical gaps, the disconnect between thought and social expectation—rather than in individual utterances. When one character challenges another, it is not overlapping talk but a cool dismantling of a flawed premise, followed by silence that lets the unspoken implications hang.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ The Body in Speech
Intercut dialogue with manual labor—breaking rocks, farming, herding cattle, sexual acts—so that speech emerges through physical exertion rather than in paused, theatrical conversation. Describe gestures and posture with anatomical or biomechanical precision but total emotional detachment, noting the angle of a wrist, the physics of a slap, or a character scratching, spitting, or performing mundane biological maintenance while delivering a profound or shocking statement. Use spatial positioning within specific landscapes (rice paddies, mountainsides, cattle sheds, cramped dormitories) to replace emotional subtext, letting the geometry of bodies in space carry the weight of unspoken tension. When characters face authority, depict the body in exaggerated, mocking compliance—standing at rigid, unnatural attention—while the mouth delivers calm subversion. DO NOT use subtle emotional tics like trembling hands or tear-filled eyes; the body is a heavy, unglamorous object dragged through time, reacting to pain or pleasure with a shrug, and what it reveals is often contrary to the speech—highlighting hypocrisy or the sheer comedy of human embodiment.}}
