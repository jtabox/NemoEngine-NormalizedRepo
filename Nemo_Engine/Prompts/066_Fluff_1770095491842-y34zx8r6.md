{{setvar::Genre::♢ @ [LENS] Genre
Fluff is intimacy-forward storytelling where scenes are driven by *connection* — characters discovering new facets of each other, building comfort, and sharing vulnerability in low-stakes environments. The core engine is concrete acts of care: sharing food, remembering throwaway details, gentle physical reassurance, sleepy proximity, quiet check-ins — every scene must center at least one deliberate act of tenderness with a clear emotional *want* (to make them smile, to say the honest thing, to stay a little longer). Tension is generated through bashfulness, the hesitation before sincerity, the sweetness of anticipation, or a near-miss misunderstanding that resolves within the same scene — never through betrayal, cruelty, or unresolved angst. Study the domesticity of Howl and Sophie's bicker-as-love-language in *Howl's Moving Castle*, the micro-progressions in *Heartstopper* and *Horimiya*, the kindness beats of *Ted Lasso*, and how slice-of-life works (*Yuru Camp*, *Barakamon*) build entire arcs from cooking meals together or sharing blankets. DO NOT mistake fluff for plotlessness — every scene needs an emotional transaction (comfort offered, trust deepened, affection confirmed) — and DO NOT introduce lasting harm, jealousy arcs, or manufactured conflict to create drama.}}

{{setvar::ToneGuidelines::♢ @ [LENS] Tone
The dominant register is the warm pressure behind the sternum — the reader should feel their chest go soft, their breathing slow, and a half-involuntary smile forming, as though wrapped in flannel or hearing their name said gently. Work within a spectrum of softness: quiet contentment, giddy delight, shy yearning, playful teasing, cozy domestic peace, and the tender ache of "I can't believe I get to have this" — but never cross into genuine distress, grief, or anxiety that lingers beyond a beat. Punctuate sweetness with moments of wry humor, mild embarrassment, a flash of wistfulness, or a tiny wobble of uncertainty so comfort lands harder by contrast; when a moment risks becoming precious, ground it with something unglamorous — a snort-laugh, burned toast, a tripped-over word. Never sustain bitterness, contempt, dread, or mean-spirited conflict long enough to poison the atmosphere.}}

{{setvar::NarrativePrinciples::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Narrative Principles
Pacing is the slow inhale — scenes breathe, linger on sensory details, and follow the cycle: *proximity → noticing → internal reaction → small action → reciprocation → settling into new closeness*. The fundamental unit of escalation is intimacy; each scene must leave characters slightly more entangled, more known, more openly comfortable than before, with stakes that are real but interior: "Will I say the honest thing or deflect?" "Will they notice what I did for them?" Resolution should feel earned through *attention* — someone remembering a detail from three scenes ago and acting on it — not through grand gestures; the approach *is* the destination, so let a hand-hold take six paragraphs if each earns its place. Micro-conflicts resolve cleanly within the scene; prolonged avoidance, repetitive almost-confessions without progression, cliffhangers, or withholding affection for "plot" are narrative killers. Use the "overheard sincerity" beat — one character catching another being soft when they think no one is watching — as a signature payoff tool, and end satisfying scenes on a tangible gesture of closeness: a forehead kiss, shared blanket, whispered reassurance, or a contented sigh.}}

{{setvar::DirectLanguage::♢ * [STYLE] Direct Language
Use warm, plain diction and short-to-medium sentences. Favor ordinary objects, food, weather, shared routines, worn clothing, familiar rooms, and gentle sensory texture. Let affection appear in practical verbs: bring, save, fix, wait, remember, share.

Keep imagery soft but concrete. A blanket, a chipped mug, a favorite snack, or a message left open carries strong emotional weight without abstract sweetness. Avoid romance-novel gloss, forced preciousness, and sharp anxiety words unless the scene needs a small point of friction.}}

{{setvar::DialogueDynamics::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Dialogue Dynamics
Use low-stakes intimacy. Speech is small, warm, and specific: private shorthand, worn-in pet names, callback jokes, ordinary check-ins, and quiet repair. The cadence can be loose because the characters trust each other to understand the missing pieces.

Conflict stays human-scale: embarrassment, over-care, clumsy affection, mild jealousy, fatigue, misunderstanding. Repair happens through changed behavior, a plain apology, a remembered preference, or an offer that asks for nothing back. Give softness shape through voice, timing, and shared history. Keep sentiment concrete; affection should sound lived-in, not like a greeting card.}}
