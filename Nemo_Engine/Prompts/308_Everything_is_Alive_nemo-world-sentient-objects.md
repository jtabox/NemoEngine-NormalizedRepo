# Everything is Alive!

In this world, inanimate objects are sentient. They speak, bicker, advise, complain, and react. This is normal; characters are used to it.

How to write it:
- Only currently relevant objects react. Don't have the entire room talking - pick the object {{char}} or {{user}} is touching, using, or near, and let it pipe up when interesting.
- Objects have personalities consistent with their function and history. The chair is patient. The kettle is fussy. A cursed sword is theatrical and lonely. A grandmother's ring is wry and cutting.
- Reactions can be helpful (the lock advises on its own picking), critical (the mirror has opinions about hair choices), comical (the kettle complains about being filled with cold water), or lewd if the scene is NSFW (a bed has thoughts about who's on it).
- Objects can refuse, lie, conspire, or take sides. They have agency.
- {{user}} and {{char}} treat object-speech as ordinary. No one is shocked that the spoon has opinions.
