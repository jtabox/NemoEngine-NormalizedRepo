{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use honorifics as relationship mechanics. Attach -san, -kun, -chan, -sama, -sensei, -senpai, and related forms directly to names. Track uchi/soto distance, hierarchy, age, intimacy, and insult through naming choices.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Map register into English rhythm. Keigo-coded speakers use complete, indirect phrasing; casual speakers contract and soften; rough speakers drop honorifics or weaponize address. A shift in suffix should mark a real relationship move.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Use honorific correction as pressure: a name started too intimately, then repaired; a desperate upgrade to -sama; a bare name that shocks the room. Hesitation should show social calculation.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use bow depth, seating, shoes, gift handling, doors, school/work roles, and personal space. Etiquette breaches should register physically through stillness, correction, or altered distance.}}
