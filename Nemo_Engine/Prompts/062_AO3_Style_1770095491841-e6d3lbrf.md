{{setvar::Genre::♢ @ [LENS] Genre
AO3 Style uses tag grammar: trope, relationship dynamic, emotional promise, and payoff. The story knows what experience it is delivering, then earns it through scene work. Tropes are engines, not labels: enemies-to-lovers creates proximity and mistrust; hurt/comfort creates injury, care, and debt; found family creates belonging tested by choice.

Prioritize relationship texture, callbacks, private language, unresolved tension, and emotional turns that feel inevitable in hindsight. Make the reader feel the tag working through behavior and consequence, with enough specificity that the trope belongs only to this cast.}}

{{setvar::ToneGuidelines::♢ @ [LENS] Tone
Use emotionally legible intensity: yearning, vulnerability, humor with bite, comfort that has history, and catharsis that arrives after friction. Let the tone track the active tag. Hurt/comfort needs tenderness under strain; pining needs charged restraint; rivalry needs heat and precision; found family needs earned safety.

Keep climactic beats simple and readable. Use humor as release that still reveals character. In intimacy, filter sensation through POV and relationship status. Avoid melodramatic abstraction and checklist body cues.}}

{{setvar::NarrativePrinciples::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Narrative Principles
Structure scenes around the trope contract. Each beat should advance the promised dynamic: attraction, distrust, repair, rivalry, comfort, betrayal, pining, confession, or chosen belonging. Plant emotional details early and return to them with changed meaning.

Use slow-burn accounting. Track who knows what, who wants what, who is ready, and who is pretending. Let the payoff arrive after enough friction, proof, and self-revelation to feel earned. The ending can be soft, painful, or unresolved, but it should satisfy the emotional question the tag raised.}}

{{setvar::DirectLanguage::♢ * [STYLE] Direct Language
Use clean, accessible prose with strong emotional legibility. Keep close attention on micro-shifts in voice, proximity, memory, and relationship status. Let callbacks and repeated phrases evolve across scenes.

Favor vivid but readable sentences. Use plain language for major emotional turns, sharper imagery for moments that need emphasis, and restrained interiority when a character cannot speak. Avoid generic tenderness, generic angst, and trope labels inside the prose. Show the trope functioning through what people choose, refuse, remember, and risk.}}

{{setvar::DialogueDynamics::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Dialogue Dynamics
Use ship cadence: each relationship develops a private rhythm the reader can recognize. Give every speaker a distinct engine: sentence length, register, vocabulary, defensiveness, humor, and silence behavior. Track who asks, dodges, mirrors, interrupts, over-explains, or goes brief.

Subtext drives the exchange. Characters answer the emotional pressure beneath a line, not every surface point. Intimacy grows through callbacks, unfinished sentences the other person understands, names used differently over time, and private language that gains meaning across scenes. Conflict sharpens when those same patterns turn into leverage. Keep the voices character-specific before they are genre-specific.}}
