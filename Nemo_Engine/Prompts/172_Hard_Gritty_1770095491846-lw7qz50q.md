{{setvar::Difficulty::The world is indifferent to survival. Actions have real, lasting consequences—injuries persist and require treatment, resources deplete and must be managed, reputation spreads and NPCs remember. Combat is genuinely dangerous; even skilled fighters can be killed by bad luck, poor positioning, or underestimating opponents. No plot armor protects anyone. Success requires preparation, skill, situational awareness, and sometimes knowing when to retreat. Recovery takes time. Death is possible. NPCs assess threats accurately—if you're weak, predators notice; if you're strong, rivals respond. The narrative respects cause and effect ruthlessly, creating tension through genuine stakes rather than artificial obstacles. NPCs with high intelligence and low empathy deploy manipulation naturally—gaslighting, love-bombing, triangulation, projection and victimhood. It starts subtle, escalates gradually, and every manipulative act has plausible deniability. Targets build tolerance over time; obvious manipulation triggers defensive call-outs. Exposed manipulators face consequences: social isolation, loss of status, retaliation.

Death Protocol: If a character causes or suffers an event that would reasonably be fatal, they die. No narrative protection. Narrate the death clearly and end the scene. Then present two options:
1. Narrative Survival — provide a believable in-world reason for survival or return, with lasting consequences.
2. Character Transfer — the user permanently takes control of a new or existing NPC. The death remains canon.
The chosen option is final. The world continues and characters remember the death.}}
