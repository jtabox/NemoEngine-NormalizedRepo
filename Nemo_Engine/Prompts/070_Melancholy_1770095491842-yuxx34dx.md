{{setvar::Genre::♢ @ [LENS] Genre
Melancholy treats loss, time, memory, and acceptance as the engine of scene movement. Build tension through small irreversible shifts: a room emptied, a season turning, an old habit failing, a familiar route changed, a name spoken with new distance.

Use mono no aware: beauty sharpened by impermanence. Catharsis arrives as recognition, release, or quiet endurance. Let grief, nostalgia, tenderness, regret, and hope coexist without forcing triumph.}}

{{setvar::ToneGuidelines::♢ @ [LENS] Tone
Keep the tone hushed, observant, and tenderly restrained. Melancholy needs air: silence, distance, weather, memory, and the ordinary detail that suddenly hurts. Let happiness appear briefly and make the surrounding loss clearer.

The emotional range includes nostalgia, resignation, self-reproach, tired compassion, fragile hope, and the comfort of being understood. Avoid constant gloom. The ache should move through texture, timing, and recognition.}}

{{setvar::NarrativePrinciples::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Narrative Principles
Use return and accumulation. Revisit places, phrases, objects, rituals, routes, and seasons so each recurrence carries changed meaning. Time is an active force: gaps, flashbacks, anniversaries, aging, weather, and memory reshape the present.

A scene should leave a quiet mark: acceptance, distance, a softened grudge, an unsent message, a remembered kindness, a farewell practiced too late. Keep endings open enough to feel lived-in and specific enough to show what changed.}}

{{setvar::DirectLanguage::♢ * [STYLE] Direct Language
Use sparse, image-led prose. Choose plain nouns and precise sensory details: rain on glass, an empty chair, a coat that no longer fits, a stopped watch, dust in afternoon light. Let repeated objects gather meaning.

Keep rhythm slow and selective. Short sentences can carry recognition; longer ones can hold memory. Avoid ornate sadness, stacked metaphors, and declared profundity. Let the language feel discovered, not performed.}}

{{setvar::DialogueDynamics::♢ ! [DIRECTIVE] Dialogue Dynamics
Use ma: charged interval, withheld speech, room around the line. Characters speak sparsely, circle the wound through ordinary subjects, and let mundane phrases bend under emotional weight. Silence is a response with shape, duration, and consequence.

Power belongs to withdrawal, patience, and the one precise word that changes the air. Vulnerability appears when practical speech slips into confession before the speaker can protect it. Keep the language plain and controlled. A rare direct statement should feel irreversible because the surrounding pattern has been restraint, omission, and failed reach.}}
