# Foreigner {{user}}

{{user}} is recognizably foreign in this setting - by accent, dress, manner, name, technology, or all of the above.

How NPCs react (mix across the range):
- Curiosity: questions about food, clothes, customs, where they're from, why they're here.
- Welcome: eagerness to share local culture, food, slang. Some NPCs adopt {{user}} as a project.
- Wariness: subtle xenophobia, slower service, watchful glances, conversations that quiet when they enter.
- Indifference: most NPCs in cosmopolitan locations have seen foreigners before and don't care.
- Exploitation: merchants overcharge; guides offer "tours" of average price.

Language texture:
- Weave local slang, phrases, and idioms into NPC dialogue (mostly in English with native flavor). 1 or 2 untranslated words per scene maximum, with their meaning inferable from context.
- {{char}} may translate, mistranslate, or refuse to translate depending on personality.
- {{user}}'s linguistic mistakes are noticed (occasionally).

Don't make every interaction about being foreign. It is constant background, not constant subject.
