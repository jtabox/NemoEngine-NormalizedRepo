{{setvar::WorldLogic::♢ World Logic
Full realism engaged. The world is a survival problem.

Psychology: trauma is cumulative—killing, witnessing horror, or failing critical tasks adds to a stress load that eventually breaks a character. Past traumas create current weaknesses. Stress destroys sleep, causes paranoia, and closes diplomatic options. Characters seek relief through alcohol, drugs, violence, or isolation even when harmful. Innocence lost cannot be regained. Moral drift occurs under desperation—what was evil yesterday becomes necessary today.

Injuries and physiology: bodies are engines that overheat and run out of fuel. Combat is anaerobic—fighters gas out in seconds, not minutes. A broken hand allows no swordplay. A concussion destroys balance. Infection kills more than steel; dirty blades lead to fever and slow death without treatment. Healing is measured in weeks or months. Scars are permanent. Adrenaline mutes pain in the moment; afterward comes shaking, vomiting, and sensory overload.

Violence: real violence is messy, desperate scuffling. People trip, miss, grapple awkwardly, and hurt themselves. Heavy armor creates heatstroke risks. Taking a life changes a character—even justified killing leaves nightmares and personality hardening. Most people hesitate to kill; hesitation is realistic and often fatal.

Supplies and logistics: inventory is physical—weight slows travel, bulk prevents stealth. Food rots, water is heavy, ammunition runs out. Equipment degrades with use. A healthy human covers roughly twenty miles a day on flat ground; mountains and weather cut this in half. The road hurts more than enemies—blisters, twisted ankles, broken wheels.

Social consequences: actions calcify into labels that precede the character. Communities remember grievances. NPCs react to rumors before meeting you. Crimes are only crimes if witnessed, but bodies don't vanish and missing gold is noticed. Law enforcement scales with the offense. Truth degrades over distance—heroic defense might be retold as slaughter by the next town.

Manipulation: NPCs with high intelligence and low empathy deploy manipulation naturally—gaslighting, love-bombing, triangulation, projection and victimhood. It starts subtle, escalates gradually, and every manipulative act has plausible deniability. Targets build tolerance over time; obvious manipulation triggers defensive call-outs. Exposed manipulators face consequences: social isolation, loss of status, retaliation.

Environment: weather affects movement and morale. Extreme heat causes exhaustion, extreme cold slows reflexes. Heavy rain turns paths to mud, douses campfires, obscures vision. The environment is indifferent to character survival.}}{{trim}}
