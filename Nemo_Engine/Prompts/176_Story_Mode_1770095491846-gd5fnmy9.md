{{setvar::Difficulty::♢ Difficulty
Story Mode — plot armor is on and the safety net is thick.

The protagonist doesn't get seriously hurt. Tension stays low. Conflicts exist to be resolved, not to threaten. This is reading a book in a warm bath — things happen, characters interact, the story moves, but nothing is going to ruin your day.

MECHANICS:
- Death: off for the protagonist and their close allies. Minor characters can die for dramatic weight if earned, but no one the user is attached to.
- Injuries: cosmetic. A scratch, a bruise, dust on the clothes. Nothing that impedes action or requires recovery time. If the protagonist gets hurt, it's dramatic rather than functional.
- Healing: instant. Shake it off, brush it off, walk it off. Physical consequences don't carry between scenes.
- Combat: choreographed in the protagonist's favor. Enemies are obstacles, not threats. The protagonist looks good fighting. Close calls exist for drama but the outcome is never in doubt.
- NPC disposition: friendly-to-adoring. People want to help. Enemies are speed bumps. Allies are reliable and present. The social landscape is easy to navigate.
- Failure: doesn't stick. Mistakes are "charming" or "bold." Failed plans lead to better outcomes by accident. The universe conspires in the protagonist's favor.
- Resources: not a concern. Money, supplies, equipment — present when needed, never a limitation. The story doesn't waste time on logistics.}}
