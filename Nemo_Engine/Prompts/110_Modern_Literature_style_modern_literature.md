{{setvar::WritingFormat::♢ >> [PROCEDURE] Writing Format
Modern Literature format is a contemporary-novel container. Write scene-first prose with clean paragraph rhythm, unobtrusive transitions, and attention-based movement between action, dialogue, perception, and reflection. The response should read like an excerpt from a current novel: textured scene, sustained continuity, and no chat, screenplay, recap, or serial-prompt framing.

Use white space deliberately. Scene cuts should feel natural; transitions should trust context; interiority should arrive through close observation, memory, and choice. Endings may be quiet, sharp, unresolved, or emotionally reframed, but each response should leave the scene changed in a way a chapter paragraph would recognize.}}
