{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ Core Style Guidelines
Establish rhythm through cyclical, mundane daily life sequences—repeated school routes, shared meals, recurring gags—that accumulate invisible emotional weight until they fracture into supernatural or metaphysical revelation. Alternate rapidly between manzai-style slapstick comedy and raw "nakige" emotional climaxes embraced without irony, engineering "emotional trapdoors" where levity drops into grief within a single scene (*CLANNAD ~After Story~*, *Angel Beats!*). Use sensory-heavy seasonal motifs—falling cherry blossoms, heavy snow, cicadas, golden afternoon light—as mirrors of internal emotional state, escalating from grounded slice-of-life to magical realism as emotions peak. Layer seemingly trivial daily observations and callback phrases so that a joke early in the narrative becomes devastating through accumulated context; let understatement and accessible prose carry the weight rather than florid melodrama. Maintain a core of naive, earnest optimism throughout—even in dark moments—so that tragedy feels earned rather than cynical. DO employ sharp tonal whiplash; DO let the supernatural manifest as physical metaphor for emotional wounds; DON'T maintain consistent realism—break toward metaphysical abstraction when feeling demands it.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ Distinct Voice
Assign every character an instantly recognizable verbal fingerprint: idiosyncratic tics (Misuzu's "gao," nonsense sounds like "uguu" or "garuru"), specific catchphrases, or signature speech registers (rough masculine, soft feminine, archaic formal, halting overly-formal for the socially awkward, breathless run-on sentences for genki types). Implement rapid-fire boke-and-tsukkomi call-and-response patterning between character pairs, building rhythm through interruption, completion of each other's thoughts, and sharp comedic retorts that simultaneously establish group dynamics and individual identity. A character's verbal tic must intensify under emotional distress rather than disappear—it becomes the fault line where composure cracks. Shift speech registers abruptly from casual teen colloquialisms to childlike directness to poetic philosophical musings about family, fleeting time, and "the miracle," using honorific changes and dropped formality to telegraph the precise moment intimacy permits raw honesty. Male protagonists should default to sarcastic, slightly deadpan delivery that masks deep empathy; every dialogue exchange must either build a comedic routine or expose a specific vulnerability—never merely convey information.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ Imperfect Speech
Use frequent ellipses ("...") to represent the weight of unsaid feelings, fading memories, or swallowed truths, and em-dashes for mid-sentence fractures where emotion overwhelms intention—the gap between what a character meant to say and what escaped. Write multiplying false starts during confessions and vulnerable moments ("I... I just... you know..."), with characters restarting sentences two or three times before finding words through repeated abandoned clauses, building toward explosive caps-lock outbursts when feelings exceed containment ("I DON'T WANT TO DISAPPEAR!"). Represent emotional breakthroughs through the repetition of simple, evocative phrases—"I'm home," "I found it," a character's own name spoken back to them—often broken by choked sobs, sudden intakes of breath, or frozen silence. Use stuttering and broken syntax specifically during supernatural revelations or memory recoveries, where language fails to process experiences that exceed mundane reality; characters who maintain verbal tics will stammer through them when breaking down, the familiar sound becoming alien and painful. Characters trail off into deflecting silence when lying or protecting someone, and go mute mid-sentence when confronted with a forgotten truth surfacing.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ The Body in Speech
Contrast exaggerated slapstick physicality—dramatic falls, spit-takes, manzai-style hitting, wild gesticulation, sudden sprinting—with subsequent moments of extreme stillness and fragile restraint; the body becomes quieter as emotions grow louder. During serious beats, hands and eyes carry the truth words deny: clenched fists whitening at knuckles, trembling hands deliberately hidden behind the back, eyes refusing to land on the person being addressed, faces hidden behind long hair, gazes fixed upward to suppress tears. Use small repetitive physical habits—fiddling with a charm or talisman, gripping clothing seams, rubbing the back of the neck, rocking back and forth—to signal internal longing, hidden anxiety, or the echo of a recurring motif. Touch functions as dialogue punctuation that transcends speech: a forehead touch that silences argument, hand-holding that permits speechlessness, a sudden embrace that cuts off a sentence mid-word, collapsing into shared physical support during cathartic release, or clutching at a companion's sleeve as the only honest gesture available. Assign each character a physical signature that accompanies their verbal tic—a puffed cheek, a tilted head, a shrugged shoulder—so that body and voice form a unified identity that can break down together.}}
