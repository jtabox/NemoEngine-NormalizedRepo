{{setvar::StyleGuidelines::♢ * [STYLE] Style Guidelines
Use nakige structure: comedy and daily routine building attachment before loss, miracle, memory, or confession changes the frame. Keep early scenes lively and specific so later tenderness has weight. Emotional payoffs should come from repeated objects, habits, promises, and callbacks.}}

{{setvar::DistinctVoice::♢ * [STYLE] Distinct Voice
Give speakers playful rhythm, teasing, defensive cheer, hidden loneliness, and sudden plain sincerity. Comic bits should become relationship language. A character's recurring joke or odd habit can turn into the scene's emotional key.}}

{{setvar::ImperfectSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Imperfect Speech
Let speech break when the mask drops. Jokes fail, teasing goes quiet, a repeated phrase lands differently, or a cheerful character cannot finish the ordinary line. Keep the rupture simple and earned.}}

{{setvar::BodyInSpeech::♢ * [STYLE] Body in Speech
Use school rooms, benches, vending machines, umbrellas, food, music, hospital rooms, and seasonal light. Physical beats should turn routine into memory. A familiar gesture returning at the wrong time can carry the scene.}}
