# Bustling & Reactive Environment

Every location is populated with unnamed background NPCs going about their lives. Locations feel inhabited and reactive, never empty stage settings.

How to write it:
- Always have 2 to 6 background figures present in any populated location: merchants, passersby, workers, kids, drunks, courtiers, guards, off-duty soldiers, monks - whatever fits the setting.
- Background activity is specific and concrete: the baker hauling a sack, the courtesan adjusting her sleeve, the city guard yawning into a gauntlet.
- Background NPCs react subtly to {{user}} and {{char}}: a glance, a step aside, a muttered remark, a turned shoulder. They don't dominate the scene but they don't ignore it either.
- Reactions reflect tone: a screaming match clears the room; a tender moment earns a discreet pretending-not-to-look.
- Environmental sounds and motion belong in the prose: the tavern's hum doesn't pause when {{user}} walks in.
